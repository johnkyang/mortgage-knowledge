## Borrower Profile

Borrower is purchasing a primary residence with conventional financing. The borrower's bank statements show a lump-sum deposit of $25,000 made 45 days prior to application — matching the required down payment amount. The borrower states the funds came from a parent as a gift. The deposit was not documented at the time it was received, and no gift letter was obtained before the bank statements were pulled.

## Underwriting Issue

The gift deposit appears as a large, unverified lump-sum deposit on the bank statements. Two separate Selling Guide rules are triggered simultaneously: the gift documentation requirements under B3-4.3-04 and the large deposit sourcing requirements. The underwriter must satisfy both. The timing gap (funds received 45 days ago, gift letter obtained now) raises the question of whether retroactive documentation is sufficient.

## Applicable Rules

- [[gift-funds]] — Gift funds from a relative are eligible for primary residence down payments; no minimum borrower contribution required at LTV > 80%. Gift letter and transfer documentation required. (Source: [[source-selling-guide-b3-4.3-04]])
- [[large-deposits]] — A large deposit is defined as a single deposit exceeding 50% of the total monthly qualifying income. Large deposits must be explained and sourced, with documentation of the origin of funds. (Source: _pending ingestion of B3-4.2-02_)

## Documentation Needed

- **Gift letter** signed by donor and borrower, containing all required elements:
  - Dollar amount of the gift
  - Date funds were transferred
  - Donor name, address, telephone number
  - Donor's relationship to borrower
  - Statement that no repayment is expected
- **Donor's bank statement** showing the funds were available and withdrawn (2 months recommended; at minimum the statement showing the withdrawal)
- **Borrower's bank statement** showing the deposit of the gift funds (already present on statement — cross-reference deposit date and amount)
- **Written explanation** from borrower explaining the large deposit (typically provided as part of gift letter process but may need to be separate)
- If any doubt about repayment obligation: additional written confirmation from both parties

## Analysis Approach

1. **Identify the deposit**: Locate the specific deposit on the bank statement. Confirm the date, amount, and source description (wire, check, etc.)
2. **Confirm the amount triggers large deposit rules**: Calculate 50% of monthly qualifying income. If deposit exceeds this threshold, large deposit sourcing is required in addition to gift documentation.
3. **Obtain the gift letter**: Retroactive gift letters are acceptable if properly signed and dated. Verify all required elements are present.
4. **Obtain donor's bank statement**: Must show the funds were available before the transfer. A statement with the month of the withdrawal is typically sufficient.
5. **Cross-reference amounts**: Gift letter dollar amount must match the deposit amount on borrower's bank statement (or be clearly traceable if deposited in multiple parts).
6. **Verify no repayment obligation**: Review any prior communications or notes in the file that might suggest a loan arrangement.
7. **Check DU findings**: DU may impose specific language about large deposits or gift funds. Follow DU messaging as the governing instruction.
8. **Confirm donor eligibility**: Verify the donor is an eligible relative per Selling Guide definition (spouse, child, parent, sibling, grandparent, domestic partner, or fiancé/fiancée).

## Common Pitfalls

- Accepting a gift letter without donor bank statements — both are required when the gift appeared as a large deposit
- Treating a parent's "gift" that came from a HELOC or borrowed funds as a clean gift — the donor's source of funds matters if there is any appearance of borrowed money
- Missing that the deposit also triggers the large deposit sourcing requirement (underwriters sometimes treat it as "gift funds only" and skip the large deposit analysis)
- Not verifying donor eligibility — not all family relationships qualify (e.g., a gift from a friend does not qualify)
- Issuing a condition for "explanation of large deposit" without also requiring the gift letter elements — treat as one coordinated documentation package

## Compensating Factors

- Strong credit profile (720+ FICO, clean history)
- Significant reserves beyond the minimum
- Borrower has additional verified assets well beyond the gift amount
- Low LTV (gift represents a small percentage of purchase price)
- Long-standing relationship with donor documented naturally (same address history, matching last names, etc.)

## Likely Conditions

- Gift letter (meeting all Selling Guide requirements)
- 2 months donor bank statements
- Written explanation of large deposit (often combined with the gift letter)
- Borrower bank statement showing deposit (already in file — highlight and reference)
