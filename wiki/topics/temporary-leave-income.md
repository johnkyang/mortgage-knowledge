## Summary

Borrowers on temporary leave (maternity/parental leave, short-term medical disability, or other employer-permitted leave) may still qualify. The underwriter must determine whether the borrower will return to work before or after the first mortgage payment date, as this drives how qualifying income is calculated. Furloughed or laid-off borrowers do NOT qualify under temporary leave guidelines.

## Key Rules

- Temporary leave is defined as short-duration absences for maternity/parental leave, short-term medical disability, or other leave types acceptable by law or the borrower's employer. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
- **Workers placed on furlough, laid off, or unemployed due to an employer-initiated action** cannot be qualified under temporary leave guidelines — their income cannot be used. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
- The borrower's **employment and income history must meet standard eligibility requirements** — temporary leave does not bypass income stability requirements. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
- The borrower must provide **written confirmation of intent to return to work**. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## Qualifying Income — FNMA

| Return-to-Work Timing | Qualifying Income |
|----------------------|-------------------|
| Borrower returns **on or before** first mortgage payment date | Use **regular employment income** (pre-leave rate) |
| Borrower returns **after** first mortgage payment date | Use the **lesser** of temporary leave income or regular employment income |

**If temporary leave income is less than regular employment income:**
The UW may supplement the temporary leave income with available **liquid financial reserves** per guidelines. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## Qualifying Income — FHLMC

| Return-to-Work Timing | Qualifying Income |
|----------------------|-------------------|
| Returns **before or on** first payment due date | Use borrower's **pre-leave gross monthly income** |
| Returns **after** first payment due date | Use gross monthly income **being received during leave** (may be zero) |

**If income is reduced during leave:**
- May combine reduced leave income with **available liquid assets** as a supplement
- Total qualifying income may **not exceed** the borrower's pre-leave gross monthly income
- Assets used as a supplement do NOT follow the standard asset-depletion income calculation method
- Assets required for the transaction (down payment, closing costs, reserves) may NOT be used as an income supplement
(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## Documentation Requirements

### FNMA
- Employment documentation per standard guidelines
- Written confirmation from borrower of intent to return + agreed-upon return date (from borrower or obtained directly from employer/designee)
- Acceptable employer documentation: prior correspondence specifying duration of leave or expected return date, or a computer printout from the employer's or designee's system of record
- **Note**: This documentation does NOT have to comply with the Age of Documentation policy
- VVOE confirming borrower is on temporary leave (borrower is considered employed)
- Documentation of pre-leave income amount and the temporary leave income amount and duration
(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FHLMC — All Borrowers on Temporary Leave
- Documentation to verify borrower's pre-leave income and employment
- Written statement (signed letter or email from borrower) confirming intent to return to current employer and intended return date
- Documentation from current employer confirming borrower's eligibility to return after leave (e.g., employer-approved leave request, FMLA document, or employer/third-party verifier documentation)
(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FHLMC — Additional (if returning AFTER first payment date)
- Documentation of amount and duration of all temporary leave income sources being used (e.g., short-term disability, sick leave, reduced employer pay)
- Verification of all available liquid assets used as income supplement (per standard Streamlined Accept or Standard Documentation requirements)
- Written rationale explaining the qualifying income analysis
(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## Exceptions & Nuances

- **Short-term disability** — covered under this section (not Social Security disability). For Social Security disability, see [[social-security-retirement-income]].
- **Employer documentation timing** — FNMA specifically carves out that the return-date documentation does not need to comply with standard age of documentation rules.
- **FHLMC assets as supplement** — The guide explicitly states the standard asset-depletion income calculation (dividing net assets by 360 or 240 months) does NOT apply to asset supplementation for temporary leave. The full liquid asset balance can supplement income up to the pre-leave income cap.

## Common Pitfalls

- Attempting to use temporary leave guidelines for a furloughed borrower — furlough = ineligible
- Using pre-leave income without confirming the return date falls on or before the first payment date
- Forgetting that on FHLMC, if the borrower returns after the first payment date, the income used is the leave income (not pre-leave) — supplemented by assets if needed
- Not getting a written letter from the employer confirming eligibility to return — a borrower's letter alone is not sufficient
- Counting assets needed for closing as available for income supplementation (not permitted)
