## Overview

Foreclosure carries the longest waiting period of all derogatory credit events — 7 years standard, 3 years with extenuating circumstances. When extenuating circumstances reduce the waiting period, additional LTV and occupancy restrictions apply for the first 3–7 years. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-07)

---

## Key Rules

### Foreclosure Waiting Period

| Condition | Waiting Period |
|---|---|
| Standard | **7 years** from completion date of foreclosure action |
| Extenuating circumstances documented | **3 years** from completion date, with additional restrictions (see below) |

The completion date is reported on the credit report or borrower-provided foreclosure documents. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-07)

### Extenuating Circumstances — Additional Restrictions (3 to 7 Years)

When a borrower qualifies under the 3-year extenuating circumstances exception, the following restrictions apply between the 3-year mark and the standard 7-year mark:

- Maximum LTV, CLTV, or HCLTV: lesser of **90%** or the transaction maximum per the Eligibility Matrix
- **Purchase of a principal residence** is permitted
- **Limited cash-out refinances** are permitted for all occupancy types
- **Purchase of second homes or investment properties** is NOT permitted
- **Cash-out refinances** (any occupancy type) are NOT permitted

After 7 years from the foreclosure completion date, all standard guidelines apply without restriction. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-07)

### Foreclosure and Bankruptcy on the Same Mortgage

If a mortgage debt was discharged through bankruptcy, the **bankruptcy waiting periods** may apply, provided the lender obtains documentation confirming the mortgage was discharged in bankruptcy. Otherwise, the **greater of** the applicable bankruptcy or foreclosure waiting period must be applied. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-07)

### What Counts as a Foreclosure on the Credit Report

Foreclosures are identified via:
- MOP (manner of payment) code of "8" on a mortgage tradeline
- Remarks Codes indicating foreclosure (e.g., "Foreclosure")

Mortgage accounts reviewed include: first liens, second liens, home improvement loans, HELOCs, and manufactured home loans.

Deed-in-lieu and preforeclosure sales are **not** identified as foreclosures. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-07, B3-5.3-09)

### Re-establishing Credit After Foreclosure

After a foreclosure, credit is considered re-established when ALL of the following are met:

1. The applicable waiting period and additional requirements are met
2. The loan receives a DU Approve/Eligible recommendation or, if manually underwritten, meets minimum credit score requirements
3. The borrower has **traditional credit** — nontraditional credit or "thin files" are not acceptable

(Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-07)

### DU Handling of Foreclosures

DU applies the following logic:

- If a foreclosure is reported within the **7-year period prior to the credit report date** → **Ineligible recommendation**
- If the filed and satisfied dates are both unknown but the foreclosure appears to be within 7 years → lender must confirm the foreclosure did not occur within the most recent 7 years
- DU assumes the date reported on the tradeline is the date of the foreclosure sale or liquidation
- DU waits for the lender to confirm all foreclosures are satisfied

**Correcting Inaccurate Foreclosure on Credit Report:**
Enter `Confirmed CR FC Incorrect` in the online application. The lender must document: the foreclosure was completed ≥7 years from disbursement, OR the account was not subject to foreclosure.

**Foreclosure Due to Extenuating Circumstances:**
Enter `Confirmed CR FC EC` in the online application. The lender must document: the foreclosure was due to EC, was completed ≥3 years from disbursement, and the loan meets all other EC requirements. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-09)

## Exceptions & Nuances

### Timeshare Accounts

Timeshare accounts are considered installment loans and are **not subject to foreclosure waiting periods** even if reported as mortgage tradelines. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-07)

### High LTV Refinance Loans

Significant derogatory credit event waiting period requirements (including foreclosure) are **not applicable** to high LTV refinance loans (see B5-7-02). (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-07)

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ The 90% LTV cap in the extenuating circumstances 3–7-year window is a combined cap with the Eligibility Matrix — the maximum is the LESSER of 90% or the applicable program max. Programs like HomeReady or ARM financing that may have lower LTV limits will further restrict.

⚠️ "Completion date" of foreclosure is critical and can be difficult to document in states with non-judicial foreclosure processes. DU assumes the credit report tradeline date; lenders should obtain actual foreclosure completion documentation when the timing is ambiguous.

⚠️ Second home purchases are blocked until 7 years post-foreclosure, even with extenuating circumstances. This is stricter than many originators realize.
