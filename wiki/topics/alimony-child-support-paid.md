## Overview

When a borrower is required to pay alimony, child support, or separate maintenance under a final divorce decree, legally binding separation agreement, or other written legally binding agreement, and payments continue for more than 10 months, they must be considered recurring monthly debt obligations. (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.251–252)

---

## Key Rules

### Documentation Required

- (Lender note: A copy of the final divorce decree, legally binding separation agreement, or other written legally binding agreement — such as a court order — is required to document the monthly obligation amount) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.252)

### 10-Month Remaining Term Rule

- (Lender note: Monthly alimony, child support, or separate maintenance payments with 10 months or fewer remaining are excluded from qualifying ratios. The mortgage file must evidence that 10 or fewer payments remain) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.252)
- (Lender note: Voluntary payments are not included in qualifying the borrower) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.252)

### Alimony with More Than 10 Months Remaining — FNMA vs. FHLMC Treatment

| GSE | Treatment |
|---|---|
| FNMA | Qualifying income is **permitted (but not required)** to be reduced by the alimony amount. If income is not reduced, alimony payment is included as a monthly liability in DTI |
| FHLMC | Qualifying income **must be reduced** by the alimony amount instead of including it as a monthly payment in DTI |

(Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.252)

### Child Support with More Than 10 Months Remaining

- (Lender note: Child support payments with more than 10 months remaining are included in monthly liabilities — there is no income-offset option as there is with alimony) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.252)

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ The FNMA vs. FHLMC difference is significant for borrowers with high alimony obligations. Under FHLMC, the alimony must reduce gross qualifying income — which affects the income side of the DTI rather than the debt side. The mathematical result is the same, but how it is entered in the AUS differs.

⚠️ "Voluntary payments" that are not court-ordered are excluded entirely — even if the borrower consistently pays them and they appear on bank statements.

⚠️ Child support received (not paid) is handled under income policy, not liabilities. This page covers only the paid obligation.
