## Overview

The credit inquiry section of the borrower's credit report must be reviewed to determine the number of inquiries within the last 90 days. Multiple unrelated inquiries represent higher credit risk regardless of whether new credit was actually obtained. (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.314–316)

---

## Key Rules

### Letter of Explanation (LOE) Requirements

- (Lender note: A condition must be added requiring a written LOE from the borrower for ALL credit inquiries within 90 days of the credit report date, or the date of any subsequent credit report pulled for the loan transaction) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.314)
- (Lender note: It must be verified that any recent debts were NOT obtained to satisfy any part of the borrower's required funds to close, including down payment, closing costs, and/or reserves) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.314)
- (Lender note: The borrower must qualify with all debt, including new or additional debt resulting from inquiries) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.314)

### Exceptions to LOE Requirement

- (Lender note: **loanDepot inquiries**: If inquiries from loanDepot are listed, the underwriter must research and document the current status of those inquiries — no LOE required from borrower) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.315)
- (Lender note: **No inquiries reported**: If no inquiries are listed on the credit report, a signed inquiry letter is not required) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.315)

### DU Inquiry Analysis (FNMA)

- (Lender note: DU evaluates inquiries made within the most recent 12 months of the credit report date) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.315)
- (Lender note: Multiple inquiries from different mortgage lenders — or different auto loan creditors within the same time frame — are NOT viewed by DU as multiple inquiries, as these reflect rate shopping) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.315)
- (Lender note: A borrower who has frequently applied for or obtained new/additional credit represents a higher risk) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.315)

### LPA Inquiry Analysis (FHLMC)

- (Lender note: LPA evaluates credit report inquiries. When underwriting with FICO scores, LPA may issue a reason code alerting that the number of inquiries affected the borrower's FICO score) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.315)

### Excessive Inquiries — Risk Factors to Consider

When several inquiries appear in the most recent 12 months, consider: (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.115)
- The borrower's payment history
- The age of the borrower's other credit
- The type of credit being sought
- The total amount of credit outstanding
- The overall credit utilization reflected on the report

Excessive inquiries combined with high revolving balances-to-limits may indicate the borrower is in danger of becoming overextended. (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.315)

### Additional Considerations

- (Lender note: Undisclosed Debt Monitoring (UDM) is used to identify credit activity that develops into a tradeline. UDM does NOT replace the credit inquiry LOE requirements) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.316)

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ The 90-day LOE window is a loanDepot overlay. Some lenders apply this to 120-day windows. Confirm the applicable window is defined by the credit report pull date, not the application date.

⚠️ Rate-shopping exemption (mortgage and auto inquiries) is built into DU logic per FNMA guidelines, but the LOE condition still applies at loanDepot. The LOE addresses whether new debt was taken — rate-shopping inquiries that resulted in no new debt would still require an LOE but would be low-risk.
