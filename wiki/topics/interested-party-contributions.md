## Summary

Interested party contributions (IPCs) are purchase transaction costs typically the borrower's responsibility that are paid directly or indirectly by another party with a financial interest in the transaction. IPCs are subject to maximum limits based on occupancy type and LTV/CLTV. Contributions exceeding the maximum require a reduction to the sales price. IPCs cannot be used for down payments, reserves, or minimum borrower contribution. Undisclosed IPCs make the loan ineligible for FNMA/FHLMC delivery.

## Key Rules

### Who Are Interested Parties

- The property seller
- The builder/developer
- The real estate agent or broker
- Any affiliate who may benefit from the sale of the property and/or its sale at the highest price possible

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### Maximum IPC Limits

Calculated based on the **lower of the sales price or appraised value** (not the loan amount).

| Occupancy Type | LTV/CLTV Ratio | Maximum IPC |
|---------------|----------------|-------------|
| Primary residence or second home | Greater than 90% | 3% |
| Primary residence or second home | 75.01% – 90% | 6% |
| Primary residence or second home | 75% or less | 9% |
| Investment property | All LTV/CLTV ratios | 2% |

Notes:
- The 3% limit at >90% LTV — see FNMA B5-4.2-03 for HomePath property exceptions.
- Limits do not apply to Freddie Mac REO property concessions.
- Concessions must not exceed the borrower's actual closing costs.

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### Consequences of Exceeding IPC Limits

- Funds exceeding maximum IPCs are treated as **financing concessions** (if used for mortgage costs/closing costs) or **sales concessions** (if non-realty items).
- Contributions exceeding the maximum require the **sales price to be adjusted downward** by the excess amount.
- Sales concessions must be **deducted from the sales price** when calculating LTV and CLTV for underwriting and eligibility. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### What Counts as an IPC

All of the following are considered IPCs:
- Funds paid directly from the interested party to the borrower
- Funds flowing from an interested party through a third-party organization (including nonprofits) to the borrower
- Funds flowing to the transaction on the borrower's behalf from an interested party (including third-party orgs and nonprofits)
- Funds donated to a third party, which then provide money to pay closing costs for a specific transaction

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### What IPCs Cannot Be Used For

- Borrower's down payment
- Reserve requirement
- Minimum borrower contribution

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### Financing Concessions

Financing concessions are IPC funds used to:
- Permanently reduce the interest rate
- Fund a buydown plan (temporary payment subsidy)
- Make contributions related to mortgage financing or closing costs

Permissible prepaid items included in financing concessions:
- Interest charges (≤30 days)
- Real estate taxes covering post-settlement period (only if escrowed)
- Property insurance premiums (≤14 months)
- HOA assessments (≤12 months)
- Initial and/or renewal mortgage insurance premiums
- Escrow accruals for renewal of borrower-purchased MI

**Exception**: A legitimate prorated real estate tax credit (taxes paid in arrears) is **not** a financing concession and is not subject to IPC limits. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### Sales Concessions

Sales concessions are financing concessions exceeding the maximums above, taking the form of non-realty items:
- Cash, furniture, automobiles, decorator allowances, moving costs, other giveaways, financing concessions exceeding limits

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### Interest Rate Buydowns

When an interested party funds an interest rate buydown, the cost of the subsidy **must be included in the IPC calculation**. Confirm the current market rate (the no-points rate) versus the offered rate to determine the discount points being contributed. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### Payment Abatements

Mortgages with **payment abatements** are not eligible for financing. A payment abatement is when an interested party funds payment of PITIA on the borrower's behalf (in whole or in part) beyond the normal prepaid/escrow items at closing.

**Exception**: HOA fee payment is not an abatement if it is for **12 months or less** (treated as an IPC subject to normal limits). HOA payment for more than 12 months is considered an abatement. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### Undisclosed IPCs

Mortgages with undisclosed IPCs are **not eligible for delivery to FNMA or FHLMC**. Examples:
- Moving expenses paid outside of closing
- Fees paid by an interested party on the borrower's behalf not on the settlement statement
- "Silent" second mortgages held by the property seller
- Other contributions given to the borrower outside of closing, not disclosed on the settlement statement

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## Exceptions & Nuances

- Funds paid by the seller that are customarily the seller's responsibility per local convention are **not subject** to IPC financing concession limits.
- Gift of equity from an eligible donor is **not** considered an IPC when requirements are met — see [[gift-of-equity]].
