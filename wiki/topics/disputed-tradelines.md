## Overview

When a borrower has disputed tradelines on their credit report, DU and LPA each handle them differently. FNMA uses a two-pass DU analysis; FHLMC defers to LPA Accept findings in most cases. (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.321–322)

---

## Key Rules

### FNMA — DU Two-Pass Analysis

- (Lender note: DU first assesses risk using all tradelines, including disputed ones. If DU issues an Approve recommendation with disputed tradelines included, no further documentation or action is required — DU will issue a specific message for this scenario) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.321)
- (Lender note: If DU does NOT approve with disputed tradelines included, DU re-assesses without the disputed tradelines. If DU then issues an Approve, the disputed tradelines must be investigated to determine whether the borrower is responsible and whether the information is accurate) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.321)

**Outcomes after DU re-assessment without disputed tradelines:**

| Scenario | Action |
|---|---|
| Borrower is NOT responsible for the disputed accounts (with supporting documentation) | Loan may be delivered as a DU loan; no further action needed on disputed tradelines |
| Borrower IS responsible AND can provide documentation to disprove adverse information (e.g., canceled checks) | Loan may be delivered as a DU loan |
| Borrower IS responsible AND account/tradeline information is accurate and complete | Loan is NOT eligible for delivery as a DU loan |

(Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.321)

### DTI Impact of Disputed Tradelines (FNMA)

- (Lender note: Monthly payments for disputed tradelines must be included in the debt-to-income ratio if the accounts belong to the borrower) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.382)

### Medical Debt Exception (FNMA)

- (Lender note: Tradelines reported as medical debt are not shown in the disputed tradeline message. Underwriters are NOT required to investigate disputed medical tradelines) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.321)

### FHLMC — Disputed Tradelines

- (Lender note: For Accept Mortgages, LPA has assessed the borrower's credit reputation and determined it is acceptable) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.321)
- (Lender note: When LPA returns an incomplete status message due to multiple disputed tradelines, the loan is ineligible for LPA and the accuracy of the disputed accounts must be verified) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.321)
- (Lender note: FHLMC — Inaccurate disputed tradelines must be corrected at the repository level, the credit report rescored, and the loan resubmitted to LPA with the rescored credit report) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.322)

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ The DU two-pass logic (with vs. without disputed tradelines) is automated within DU — the UW does not manually toggle this. The UW's role is to interpret the DU message and take appropriate action based on which outcome DU reports.

⚠️ "Borrower is responsible" is a judgment call that may require documentation review. Canceled checks are cited as one example — any documentation that disproves the adverse information can be used.

⚠️ For FHLMC, the LPA incomplete status is the trigger for the manual investigation process. If LPA returns Accept (even with disputed tradelines present), no further action is required under FHLMC guidelines.
