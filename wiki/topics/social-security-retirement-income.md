## Summary

Social Security income (retirement, disability, SSI, survivor benefits) is acceptable qualifying income with documentation. A key official rule: 15% of the total monthly SS payment is treated as nontaxable automatically — **no tax return documentation is required** for this 15% portion. Grossing up more than 15% requires additional documentation. Continuance rules differ significantly by benefit type and by whether the borrower draws on their own vs. another person's account.

## Key Rules — FNMA (B3-3.4-15)

### Social Security — Income History
- **No minimum history** is required. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])

### Social Security — Continuance
- **Retirement or long-term disability** drawn on **own account/work record**: Lender is **not required to verify continuance** unless there is reason to believe income may not continue. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])
- **All other SS scenarios**: The lender must document income is expected to continue for at least **3 years from the note date**. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])
- 3-year continuance **does not require a defined expiration date** — can be confirmed by verifying SSA requirements related to the specific benefit. Example: if benefits are tied to beneficiary's age, verify age supports 3+ years of continued benefit. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])

### Social Security — Gross-Up (Nontaxable Portion)
- The lender is **not required to provide documentation** to support that 15% of Social Security income is nontaxable — this 15% is automatically assumed nontaxable. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])
- Apply a **25% gross-up factor** to the nontaxable portion:
  - Example: $1,500/mo SS → $1,500 × 15% = $225 nontaxable → $225 × 25% = $56 → qualifying income = **$1,556**
- To gross up **more than 15%**, additional documentation supporting the nontaxable amount must be included. See B3-3.1-01. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])

### DU Validation Service
- If SS income is validated by DU, follow DU message documentation requirements (may differ from standard table). (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])

## Documentation Requirements — FNMA (B3-3.4-15)

| Benefit Type | Borrower's Own Account/Work Record | From Another Person's Account/Work Record |
|---|---|---|
| Retirement | SSA Award letter, SSA-1099, most recent signed federal tax return (or transcript), or proof of current receipt | SSA Award letter + proof of current receipt + 3-year continuance |
| Disability | Same as Retirement (own account) | SSA Award letter + proof of current receipt + 3-year continuance |
| Survivor Benefits | N/A | SSA Award letter + proof of current receipt + 3-year continuance |
| Supplemental SS (SSI) | SSA Award letter + proof of current receipt | N/A |
| Beginning before first payment date | SSA Award letter required; proof of current receipt not required | Same |

_Note on joint tax returns_: If joint returns include income not associated with a borrower on the loan, obtain additional documentation (e.g., SSA-1099) confirming the SS amount being used. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])

## VA Benefits — FNMA (B3-3.4-18)

- Document income with one of: VA statement, award letter/benefit statement, bank statement, signed federal tax return, or IRS 1099.
- Also obtain evidence the borrower will receive at least one payment on or before the first payment due date.
- **No minimum history** required.
- **3-year continuance** must be documented — **except**: VA retirement or long-term disability benefits do not require continuance verification.
- **Education benefits** (G.I. Bill) are **not** acceptable income — they are offset by education expenses.
- DU Validation Service available. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])

## Annuity / Pension / Retirement — FNMA (B3-3.4-03)

- Documentation: statement from organization, retirement award letter, bank/financial account statement, signed federal tax return, W-2, or 1099.
- If payments begin on or before first payment date: benefit statement specifying income type, amount, frequency, and initial start date.
- **Fixed distribution**: No minimum history. Use fixed payment amount.
- **Variable distribution**: 12-month history required. Average of most recent 12 months.
- **Continuance**:
  - Insurance/personal annuity or retirement account distribution: 3-year continuance required.
  - Pension distributions: 3-year continuance determined using the applicable written agreement, government program documentation, or information required by law.
  - Eligible retirement account balances (401(k), IRA, Keogh) may be combined to determine if 3-year continuance is met — borrower must have unrestricted access without penalty.
(Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])

## Exceptions & Nuances

- **Short-term disability** (non-SS) — covered under [[temporary-leave-income]], not this section
- **Continuance bifurcation**: SS retirement/LTD from own record = no continuance docs needed; SS from another person's record = 3-year continuance required. This is a commonly missed distinction. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])
- **loanDepot overlay**: Pending or current medical re-evaluation of eligibility is NOT considered an indication the income will not continue. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## Common Pitfalls

- Applying the full gross-up (100% of SS as nontaxable) without documentation — only 15% is automatically nontaxable without docs; more requires supporting documentation
- Forgetting 3-year continuance for SS drawn from another person's account (survivor benefits, disability on another's work record)
- Treating SSI the same as retirement SS — SSI from own account requires proof of current receipt; retirement does not
- Using G.I. Bill educational benefits as qualifying income — explicitly ineligible under FNMA
