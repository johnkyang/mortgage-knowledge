## Summary

Vested, publicly traded stocks, bonds, mutual funds, and U.S. government securities are eligible sources of funds for down payment, closing costs, and reserves. Non-vested restricted stock units, non-vested stock options, and stocks in unlisted companies are ineligible. When assets are used for closing (down payment or closing costs), evidence of liquidation may be required unless the combined asset value exceeds the needed amount by at least 20%.

## Key Rules

### FNMA — Eligible Securities

Eligible: stocks, vested stock options, government bonds, mutual funds (all publicly traded, vested). Non-eligible: non-vested stock options, restricted stock not yet vested. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FNMA — Value Determination

| Asset Type | How to Value |
|-----------|-------------|
| Stocks and Mutual Funds | Most recent monthly or quarterly statement from depository/investment firm, **or** copy of stock certificate plus newspaper stock list dated near application date. Value must be net of any margin accounts. |
| Vested Stock Options | Statement listing number of options and option price; use current stock price to determine gain from exercise and sale. |
| Government Bonds | Based on purchase price unless the redemption value can be documented. |

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FNMA — Liquidation Evidence Requirement

- **For down payment or closing costs**: If the asset value (as determined above) is at least **20% more** than the amount of funds needed for down payment and closing costs → no liquidation documentation required. Otherwise, evidence of actual receipt of proceeds from sale/liquidation is required.
- **For reserves**: 100% of the asset value may be counted; liquidation is **not required**.

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FHLMC — Eligible Securities

Securities traded on an exchange or marketplace generally available to the public:
- Stocks, vested stock options, bonds, mutual funds, U.S. government securities, other securities

**Ineligible (FHLMC)**: Stocks with limitations on accessibility (e.g., unvested restricted stock). Value must not include margin accounts. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FHLMC — Documentation Requirements

| Documentation Level | Statement Period |
|--------------------|-----------------|
| Streamlined Accept | Account statement covering 1-month period **or** direct account verification (VOD) |
| Standard | Account statement covering 2-month period **or** direct account verification (VOD) |

If the borrower does not receive a stock/security account statement:
- Provide evidence of ownership, and
- Verify value using current stock prices from a financial publication or website.

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FHLMC — Government Bonds (Value)

Value must be based on the **lower of purchase price or redeemable value**. Provide documentation verifying ownership and value. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FHLMC — Liquidation Evidence Requirement

When assets invested in stocks, bonds, mutual funds, U.S. government securities, retirement accounts, or other securities are **needed for closing**, evidence of liquidation is required **unless** the combined value of those assets is at least **20% greater** than the amount from those assets needed for closing. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## Exceptions & Nuances

- The 20% buffer rule (no liquidation evidence needed) applies to both FNMA and FHLMC, but FHLMC applies it across all securities including retirement accounts as a combined pool.
- For FHLMC, if the borrower does not receive standard account statements, ownership plus value from a public source is acceptable.
