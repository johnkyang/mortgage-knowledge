## Overview

The underwriter must review the borrower's credit report for all mortgage accounts. FNMA requires that existing mortgages be current at application and that adequate payment history be documented. FHLMC defers to LPA Accept findings. (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.322–323)

---

## Key Rules

### FNMA — Current Mortgage Status

- (Lender note: On the date of the loan application, the borrower's existing mortgage must be current — meaning no more than 45 days may have elapsed since the last paid installment date) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.322)

### FNMA — Prior Mortgage Payment History Documentation

- (Lender note: If the credit report includes a reference to the mortgage(s) and reflects 12 months of the most recent payment activity, the underwriter does NOT need to independently verify the mortgage's payment history) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.323)
- (Lender note: If an adequate mortgage payment history is NOT included in the borrower's credit report, one of the following can be used to verify prior mortgage payment history:
  - A standard mortgage verification
  - Loan payment history from the servicer
  - The borrower's canceled checks for the last 12 months
  - The borrower's year-end mortgage account statement (if it includes a payment receipt history, plus canceled checks for months elapsed since the statement was issued)) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.323)

### FNMA — Excessive Prior Mortgage Delinquency

- (Lender note: Loans with excessive prior mortgage delinquencies are NOT eligible) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.323)
- (Lender note: Excessive prior mortgage delinquency is defined as any mortgage that has one or more 60-, 90-, 120-, or 150-day delinquency reported within the 12 months prior to the credit report date) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.323)

### FHLMC — Mortgage History

- (Lender note: For Accept Mortgages, LPA has evaluated the borrower's credit reputation and determined it is acceptable. No further documentation should be required) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.323)
- (Lender note: Direct verification of mortgage debt not shown on the credit report is typically not required for Accept Mortgages) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.323)
- (Lender note: The Uniform Residential Loan Application Declarations must coincide with the documentation within the loan file) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.323)
- (Lender note: For Accept Mortgages with borrowers who do not have a usable credit score, refer to FHLMC Guide Section 5201.1(c)) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.323)

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ The FNMA 45-day rule applies at the loan application date — not the closing date. A mortgage that is 30 days late at application would still be within the 45-day window and technically current. However, the underwriter should assess whether the delinquency represents a pattern.

⚠️ The "excessive delinquency" definition (one or more 60+ day lates in the past 12 months) is absolute — a single 60-day late in the prior 12 months makes the loan ineligible for FNMA under loanDepot's guidelines. This is stricter than some standard FNMA guidance that allows 0x30 in 12 months for certain product types.

⚠️ FHLMC's reliance on LPA findings means the 45-day and payment history requirements listed above are FNMA-specific. Do not apply the FNMA delinquency threshold to FHLMC loans.
