## Overview

Fannie Mae does not publish specific minimum tradeline count requirements for DU-underwritten loans — DU performs its own holistic credit risk analysis. For manually underwritten loans, the Selling Guide emphasizes the qualitative assessment of number, age, and type of accounts rather than prescribing a specific count. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-01)

---

## Key Rules

### FNMA — Number and Age of Accounts (Manual Underwriting)

The lender must review the borrower's credit report to determine:

- Whether the borrower has an **older, established credit history** or a **newly established credit history**
- Whether there are a significant number of **recently opened accounts** or a mix of new and older accounts

**Risk interpretation:**
- Older, established accounts → generally **lower** credit risk
- Older established history with many recently opened accounts → may indicate overextension → **higher** credit risk
- Newly established credit history → does **not** automatically mean higher risk; making timely payments on new accounts is less risky than delinquency on established accounts

(Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-01)

### FNMA — DU Loan Casefiles

For DU loans, no specific minimum tradeline count is required. DU evaluates:
- Whether any credit accounts or installment accounts exist in the credit report
- If at least one such account is present, DU uses standard eligibility guidelines
- If no accounts exist, DU applies the nontraditional credit requirements (see [[nontraditional-credit-history]])

(Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.4-01)

### Borrowers Without Any Tradelines

If no borrower has a credit account or installment account on their credit report, DU applies nontraditional credit history requirements. See [[nontraditional-credit-history]] and [[no-credit-score-borrowers]] for full eligibility rules. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.4-01)

## Exceptions & Nuances

### loanDepot Overlay — Specific Tradeline Count Requirements

(Lender note: loanDepot may impose specific minimum tradeline counts as an overlay on FNMA's qualitative standard. Check product-specific matrices for applicable tradeline requirements.) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### Manual Underwriting — Nontraditional Credit Path

When no credit score exists, borrowers without traditional tradelines may qualify using nontraditional credit references. Manual UW requires a minimum of 3 nontraditional credit references per borrower (4 for HomeReady). See [[nontraditional-credit-history]]. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.4-02)

---

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ The absence of a specific FNMA tradeline count minimum does not mean "any number is acceptable." DU weights credit history depth and age. A borrower with only one 6-month-old credit card may receive a DU Refer/Ineligible despite having no derogatory marks.

⚠️ Lenders (including loanDepot) frequently impose specific minimum tradeline counts as overlays — commonly requiring 3 tradelines open for 12+ months. These overlays are more restrictive than the FNMA floor. Always check the lender's product matrix.

⚠️ The FNMA Eligibility Matrix does not currently publish a minimum tradeline count for DU loans. For manually underwritten loans, the Eligibility Matrix may impose additional credit requirements depending on LTV, occupancy, and loan purpose.
