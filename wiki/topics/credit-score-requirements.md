## Overview

Fannie Mae requires classic FICO credit scores from all three major repositories for most loans. Minimum score requirements vary by underwriting method (DU vs. manual) and loan type. DU casefiles have no minimum score threshold — DU performs its own holistic credit risk assessment. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.1-01)

---

## Key Rules

### Required FICO Score Versions

Fannie Mae requires the following specific FICO versions for both DU and manually underwritten loans:

- **Equifax**: Beacon® 5.0
- **Experian**: Fair Isaac Risk Model V2SM
- **TransUnion**: FICO® Risk Score, Classic 04

The lender must request all three scores from each of the three major credit repositories when ordering the tri-merge credit report. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.1-01)

### Minimum Credit Score Requirements

| Transaction Type | Minimum Credit Score |
|---|---|
| Manually underwritten loans — fixed-rate | 620 (representative or average median score) |
| Manually underwritten loans — ARMs | 640 |
| DU loan casefiles | No minimum — DU performs its own risk assessment |
| Mortgage loans insured by FHA, VA, or RD | 620 (representative credit score) |
| Loans with a variance in the Lender Contract | Higher of 620 or the minimum required by the variance |

For manually underwritten loans:
- **One borrower**: representative credit score applies
- **Multiple borrowers**: average median credit score applies

The minimum score is based on the credit score AND the highest of LTV, CLTV, or HCLTV (as applicable), loan purpose, number of units, amortization type, and DTI. See the Eligibility Matrix for the full requirements. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.1-01)

### Exceptions to Minimum Score Requirements

The minimum credit score requirement does NOT apply to:

- Loans where no borrower has a credit score (see [[nontraditional-credit-history]] and [[no-credit-score-borrowers]])
- Manually underwritten HomeReady loans that include a borrower with a low credit score (see B5-6-02)
- High LTV refinance loans (except those using the Alternative Qualification Path)

(Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.1-01)

### DU-Specific Credit Score Rules

Credit scores are **not an integral part of DU's risk assessment** — DU performs its own analysis of the credit report data. However, lenders must still request scores from all three repositories.

A credit report is still acceptable for DU if:
- Credit data is available from at least **one** repository (if others have no data), AND
- The lender requested a three in-file merged report

**Frozen credit for DU**: If one repository is frozen, the report is acceptable if data is available from two repositories AND a three in-file merged report was requested. Loans with credit frozen at two or more repositories are **not eligible**, whether manually or DU underwritten. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.1-01)

### Delivery of Credit Scores

Lenders must deliver the representative credit score for all loans at Loan Delivery. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.1-01)

### Loan-Level Price Adjustments (LLPAs)

LLPAs are assessed based on the representative credit score for the loan, in addition to other loan features. See the LLPA Matrix for current pricing. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.1-01)

## Exceptions & Nuances

### loanDepot Overlay — Program-Specific Minimums

(Lender note: loanDepot product-specific program matrices may impose minimum credit score floors above FNMA's 620 baseline. Always check the applicable matrix.) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FHLMC vs. FNMA

FHLMC also uses the classic FICO score but employs LPA (Loan Product Advisor) for its risk assessment. LPA does not eliminate the credit score requirement the way DU's holistic analysis does. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.317)

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ The DU "no minimum credit score" rule is frequently misunderstood. DU does not ignore credit scores — it uses them as one data point in a broader credit risk assessment. A 580-score borrower can receive DU Approve/Eligible, but it is unusual and depends heavily on all other risk factors. There is no guarantee.

⚠️ The 620/640 manual underwrite minimums are fixed Fannie Mae floors. No extenuating circumstances exception applies to the minimum credit score requirement itself.

⚠️ The average median credit score applies only to manually underwritten multi-borrower loans for determining loan eligibility. For pricing (LLPAs), the representative credit score (lowest median) is always used regardless of underwriting method.
