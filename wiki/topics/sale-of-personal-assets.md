## Summary

Proceeds from the sale of personal assets are an acceptable source of funds for down payment, closing costs, and reserves, provided the purchaser is not an interested party to the transaction. Titled assets require ownership documentation, and when proceeds exceed 50% of monthly qualifying income (FNMA), independent value documentation is required.

## Key Rules

### General

- Proceeds from the sale of personal assets are eligible for down payment, closing costs, and reserves. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
- The individual purchasing the borrower's asset **must not be an interested party** to the real estate or mortgage transaction. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FNMA — Documentation Requirements

All of the following must be documented:
1. **Ownership**: For titled assets (e.g., automobile title), the borrower's ownership of the asset must be documented.
2. **Value** (if proceeds exceed 50% of total monthly qualifying income): The value must be determined by an **independent and reputable source**. The **lesser of** the estimated value (from the independent source) or the actual sales price is used.
   - Example: Vehicle with independent value of $10,000 sells for $12,000 → only $10,000 added to borrower's available funds.
3. **Transfer of ownership**: Evidenced by a bill of sale or statement from the purchaser.
4. **Receipt of proceeds**: Documented by deposit slip, bank statements, copy of purchaser's canceled check, or equivalent payment source.

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FHLMC — Documentation Requirements

- A **signed bill of sale** documenting the asset and the transfer of ownership, and
- **Evidence of receipt** of the proceeds.

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## Exceptions & Nuances

- The proceeds from the sale of real property (including 1031 exchanges) are a separate eligible asset type — see the [[asset-verification-documentation]] overview. Proceeds from the sale of real property require a Settlement/Closing Disclosure statement.
- Sale proceeds deposited into the borrower's account may appear as a large deposit — see [[large-deposits]].
