## Overview

Timeshare installment loans are treated as standard installment debt for DTI purposes. Timeshare maintenance fees are excluded from DTI under both GSEs. FHLMC has an additional carve-out: timeshare foreclosures do not trigger standard foreclosure seasoning requirements. (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.272–273)

---

## Key Rules

### FNMA — Timeshare Debt

- (Lender note: Timeshare installment debt must be considered part of the borrower's recurring monthly debt if there are more than 10 monthly payments remaining) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.272)
- (Lender note: Timeshare maintenance fees are NOT included as a liability in the DTI ratio) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.272)

### FHLMC — Timeshare Debt

- (Lender note: Timeshare loans are considered installment debts regardless of how they are reported on the borrower's credit report) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.272)
- (Lender note: Timeshare loans are NOT considered in evaluating late housing payments) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.272)
- (Lender note: Maintenance fees associated with timeshares are NOT required to be included in the monthly DTI) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.272)
- (Lender note: The foreclosure recovery time periods for re-establishment of credit do NOT apply to timeshare loans in foreclosure — timeshare foreclosure does not trigger standard derogatory credit seasoning requirements) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.272)
- (Lender note: Any derogatory information on a timeshare must be documented to support the underwriting decision in the mortgage file) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.272)

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ FHLMC's timeshare foreclosure carve-out is meaningful — a borrower who had a timeshare foreclosed does not face a 7-year standard foreclosure waiting period. This is an often-unknown exception that can save otherwise eligible borrowers from an unnecessary denial.

⚠️ Timeshare maintenance fees are excluded from DTI at both GSEs — but they should still appear in the borrower's bank statements, and the underwriter should assess whether they affect the borrower's residual income or ability to repay.

⚠️ On FHLMC, timeshare loans classified oddly on the credit report (e.g., as revolving or "open account") should still be treated as installment debt per this policy.
