## Overview

Loans where no borrower has a credit score are eligible under specific conditions. FNMA allows these loans through DU or manual underwriting with nontraditional credit. loanDepot adds overlays beyond the FNMA guidelines. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.4-01; [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.313)

---

## Key Rules

### FNMA — Manual Underwriting with No Credit Score Borrowers

Requirements when at least one borrower lacks a credit score and the loan will be manually underwritten:

- Property: **1-unit, principal residence**
- Transaction: **purchase or limited cash-out refinance only**
- Loan amount: **baseline conforming limits only** — high-balance loans not eligible
- Maximum DTI: **36%**
- Nontraditional credit history must be documented for each no-score borrower (see [[nontraditional-credit-history]])
- Reserve requirement: none if at least one borrower documents a housing payment as a nontraditional reference; otherwise **12 months' reserves required**
- Non-occupant co-borrowers: permitted (subject to B2-2-04)

(Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.4-01)

### FNMA — DU with No Credit Score Borrowers

When **no borrower** has at least one credit account or installment account on their credit report:

- Property: **1–4 unit, principal residence** — all borrowers must occupy
- Transaction: **purchase or limited cash-out refinance only**
- Reserves: may be required as determined by DU
- Nontraditional credit references: not required if DU conducts a cash flow assessment and issues a qualifying message; otherwise required per DU instructions (minimum 2 references per borrower)

When DU receives a Refer/Ineligible for a no-score casefile, the loan may be eligible for manual underwriting if the transaction meets manual UW requirements. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.4-01)

### When Nontraditional Credit Is NOT Acceptable

Even with no credit score, nontraditional credit cannot be used when:
- The lender can obtain a credit score despite limited credit use
- The borrower has enough credit for a score but it falls below the minimum
- The borrower's traditional credit shows significant derogatory information (prior bankruptcy, foreclosure) — must re-establish traditional credit first

(Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.4-01)

## Exceptions & Nuances

### Credit Score Exception for Minimum Score

Loans where no borrower has a credit score are **exempt from the minimum credit score requirement** (see [[credit-score-requirements]]). This exception does not apply if the borrower could obtain a score but it falls below the minimum. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.1-01)

### loanDepot Overlays on No-Credit-Score Loans

(Lender note: loanDepot imposes the following overlays beyond FNMA guidelines:
- Maximum 95% LTV/CLTV/HCLTV
- All borrowers must occupy the property (no non-occupant co-borrowers per loanDepot policy)
- Fixed-rate mortgages only
- Conforming loan amounts only
- DU Approve/Eligible required (manual UW not permitted per loanDepot policy)
- At least one borrower must complete Homeownership Education
- Property must be 1–4 units (FNMA)

The following programs are NOT eligible for no-credit-score loans at loanDepot: HomeReady, HomeStyle Renovation, HomeStyle Energy, Texas Homestead Refinance, AccessOne, HomeOne, Home Possible.) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.313)

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ The loanDepot overlay prohibiting manual UW for no-score loans is more restrictive than FNMA's guidelines. FNMA permits manual UW; loanDepot requires AUS approval. Always check the lender's current product matrix.

⚠️ The 95% LTV cap cited in loanDepot's guide is a lender overlay. FNMA does not impose a separate maximum LTV for no-score loans beyond standard program limits.

⚠️ DU's cash flow assessment (using a 12-month third-party asset verification report) can eliminate the nontraditional credit reference requirement entirely for DU loans. This represents a significant alternative path for borrowers with no credit score but substantial assets.
