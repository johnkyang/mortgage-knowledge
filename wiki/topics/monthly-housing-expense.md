## Overview

Each borrower's principal residence housing payment must be included in the housing expense-to-income ratio. For primary residence transactions, all monthly housing charges on the subject property are included. For second home, investment, and non-occupant transactions, the borrower's primary residence expense is also included. (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.264–266)

---

## Key Rules

### Primary Residence — Monthly Housing Expense Components

The following must be included in the monthly housing expense calculation: (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.264–265)

- Principal and interest payments on the mortgage
- Property hazard insurance premiums
- Real estate taxes (see [[property-tax-qualification]] for new construction and reassessment rules)
- Mortgage insurance premiums (when applicable)
- Flood insurance premiums (full risk premium including all fees and surcharges)
- Leasehold payments
- Ground rent
- Special assessments with more than 10 monthly payments remaining
- Homeowners association dues (excluding unit utility charges)
- Co-op corporation fees
- Maintenance fees (excluding unit utility charges)
- Payments on secondary financing including HELOCs

### HELOC Payment Calculation

- (Lender note: HELOC payments must be included in the monthly DTI when there is an outstanding balance on the account) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.265)
- (Lender note: FNMA: When a HELOC has an outstanding balance and no monthly payment appears on the credit report, documentation of the monthly payment must be included in the mortgage file) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.265)
- (Lender note: FHLMC: When no monthly payment appears on the credit report and there is no documentation in the file indicating a payment amount, **1.5% of the outstanding balance** will be used as the HELOC monthly payment) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.265)

### Second Home and Investment Property

- (Lender note: For second home and investment property mortgages, the monthly housing expense is the sum of the same charges as listed for primary residences) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.265)
- (Lender note: The primary residence housing expense must be included in the housing expense-to-income ratio, unless the borrower has no primary residence housing expense) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.264)
- (Lender note: Confirmation of no housing expense must be in the loan file for transactions where the borrower has no primary residence housing expense) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.264)

### Non-Occupant Borrower

- (Lender note: For mortgages with a non-occupying borrower, the monthly housing expense is the sum of monthly charges for each borrower's primary residence) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.265)

### Rent Documentation — When Borrower Rents Their Residence

For second home, investment, and non-occupant borrowers who pay rent (not a mortgage): (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.265–266)

**FNMA — Acceptable documentation:**
- Six months' canceled checks or equivalent payment source
- Six months' bank statements reflecting clear and consistent payment to an organization or individual
- Direct verification of rent from a management company or individual landlord
- Copy of a current, fully-executed lease agreement AND two months' canceled checks (or equivalent)

**FHLMC — Acceptable documentation:**
- Direct verification from a management company, OR
- Direct verification from an individual landlord, supported by two months of canceled checks or payment evidence, OR
- Copy of current, fully-executed lease agreement, supported by two months of canceled checks or payment evidence, OR
- Six months' canceled checks or bank statements supporting consistent payments in the qualifying amount

(Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.265–266)

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ FHLMC's 1.5% HELOC payment default (when no payment is documented) is more conservative than many expect. On a $100,000 HELOC balance, this creates a $1,500/month payment in DTI. Always pull documentation of the actual HELOC payment if available.

⚠️ FNMA requires documentation of the actual HELOC payment — there is no assumed percentage; the underwriter must find the payment. If it's not on the credit report, a statement or account documentation is required.

⚠️ The "no primary residence housing expense" confirmation is frequently overlooked. If a borrower owns their primary free and clear (no mortgage, no rent), documentation of $0 housing expense must be in the file.
