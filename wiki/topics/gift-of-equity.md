## Summary

A gift of equity is a credit from the property seller's equity to the buyer, representing a portion of the seller's equity transferred as a credit in the transaction. It is permitted only for principal residence and second home purchase transactions. The seller/donor may not be affiliated with any other interested party to the transaction. Gift of equity may not be used for reserves or cash back.

## Key Rules

### General

- Gift of equity is permitted for **principal residence and second home** purchase transactions only. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
- The seller/donor of the gift of equity **may not be affiliated** with any other interested party to the transaction. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
- Gift of equity **cannot be used for reserves** (both FNMA and FHLMC). (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
- Gift of equity **is not eligible** for investment property transactions. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FNMA

- A gift of equity can fund all or part of the down payment and closing costs (including prepaid items).
- The minimum borrower contribution requirements apply (see [[minimum-borrower-contribution]]).
- **Documentation required**:
  - Signed gift letter stating the actual or maximum amount of the gift.
  - Settlement statement listing the gift of equity.

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FHLMC — Eligible Donors

The donor must be a **Related Person**:
- Spouse, child, or dependent
- Individual related to borrower by blood, marriage, or adoption
- Guardian of the borrower or person for whom the borrower is a guardian
- Fiancée or fiancé
- Domestic partner
- Unrelated individual with close, family-like ties to the borrower
- A trust established by a Related Person
- The estate of a Related Person

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

**Second home special rule (FHLMC)**: When the mortgage is secured by a second home and LTV/TLTV/HTLTV > 80%, a gift of equity is permitted only if the borrower has made a down payment of at least **5% from personal funds**. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FHLMC — Documentation Requirements

- Gift letter must be signed by the donor (or trustee/authorized representative if from trust/estate), state the donor's name and relationship category, include mailing address and phone, state the actual or maximum amount, and establish that the gift does not have to be repaid.
- Receipt of the gift of equity must be reflected on the **Settlement/Closing Disclosure Statement**.

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## Exceptions & Nuances

- If the requirements in this section are met, the gift of equity is **not subject to interested party contribution (IPC) requirements**. The donor of a gift of equity is not considered an interested party to the transaction. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
- Gift of equity reduces the buyer's required funds to close (shown as a credit on the settlement statement) — it does not transfer cash and does not appear in the borrower's bank account.
