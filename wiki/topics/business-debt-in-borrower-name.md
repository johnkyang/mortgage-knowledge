## Overview

When a self-employed borrower has a monthly obligation appearing on their personal credit report that is actually paid by their business, the debt may be excluded from DTI — but only with documented confirmation. (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.255–256)

---

## Key Rules

### FNMA — Business Debt Exclusion

The account payment does NOT need to be included in the borrower's DTI if ALL of the following are met: (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.255)
- The account does not have a history of delinquency
- The business provides acceptable evidence that the obligation was paid out of company funds (e.g., 12 months of canceled company checks)
- The lender's cash flow analysis of the business took payment of the obligation into consideration

The account payment MUST be included in DTI in any of the following situations: (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.255–256)
- The business does not provide sufficient evidence of business payment
- The business provides evidence of payment, but the cash flow analysis does not reflect a business expense related to the obligation (e.g., interest expense equal to or greater than what would be reasonably expected given the financing amount and loan age) — this makes it reasonable to assume the obligation was not accounted for in the cash flow
- The account has a history of delinquency — to avoid double-counting, adjust the net income of the business by the amount of interest, taxes, or insurance related to the account

### FHLMC — Business Debt Exclusion

The debt may be excluded from the monthly DTI ratio if BOTH of the following are met: (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.256)
- The mortgage file contains evidence that the debt has been paid timely by the borrower's business for no less than the most recent 12 months
- The tax returns evidence that business expenses associated with the debt (e.g., interest, lease payments, taxes, insurance) have been reported, supporting that the debt has been paid by the business

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ FNMA's "cash flow analysis took the obligation into account" requirement means the income calculation must have already deducted or recognized the business payment — if the self-employed income worksheet doesn't show the expense, the debt cannot be excluded from personal DTI.

⚠️ FHLMC simplifies the test to: 12 months of timely business payment evidence AND tax return expense documentation. This is a cleaner two-part test than FNMA's more judgment-based standard.

⚠️ This applies only to self-employed borrowers where the business is actually paying the debt. For W-2 employees or other situations, business payment is generally not a valid exclusion basis.
