## Overview

Revolving charge accounts and unsecured lines of credit are treated as long-term debts and must be included in the borrower's recurring monthly debt obligations. These include credit cards, department store charge cards, and personal lines of credit. (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.268)

---

## Key Rules

### Payment to Use in DTI

- (Lender note: Revolving accounts must be considered part of the borrower's recurring monthly debt obligations — they are always long-term debts regardless of balance) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.268)

**When the credit report reflects a monthly payment of $1 or less:** (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.268)

| AUS | Payment to Use |
|---|---|
| DU (FNMA) | Obtain documentation supporting the payment, OR use the **greater of $10 or 5% of the outstanding balance** |
| LPA (FHLMC) | Use **5% of the outstanding balance** |

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ The $1 payment threshold applies when the credit report reflects an unusually low payment — commonly seen on accounts the borrower is paying down or accounts with minimal balances. It is not a signal that the account is paid off.

⚠️ FNMA's $10 floor means a $50 outstanding balance on a credit card would use $10 (5% = $2.50, but $10 minimum applies). FHLMC has no floor — the same $50 balance would use $2.50.

⚠️ Revolving accounts with a $0 balance and $0 reported payment do not need to be included in DTI — there is no outstanding balance to calculate a payment from.
