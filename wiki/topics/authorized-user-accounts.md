## Overview

An authorized user is a person permitted by the account owner to use a credit account. Authorized user tradelines establish credit history for the user based on the owner's payment record, even though the user is not the primary account holder. (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.252–255)

---

## Key Rules

### FNMA — Authorized User Tradeline Review

- (Lender note: DU takes authorized user tradelines into consideration in its credit risk assessment) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.253)
- (Lender note: Authorized user tradelines must be reviewed to ensure they accurately reflect the borrower's credit history) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.253)
- (Lender note: If the authorized user tradeline belongs to another borrower on the mortgage loan, no additional investigation is needed) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.253)
- (Lender note: It is NOT required to review an authorized user tradeline that belongs to the borrower's spouse when the spouse is not on the mortgage transaction) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.253)

**When to investigate FNMA authorized user tradelines:**

When the borrower has several authorized user accounts but only a few of their own, establish: (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.253)
- The relationship of the borrower to the account owner
- Whether the borrower uses the account
- Whether the borrower makes the payments on the account

If the borrower has several tradelines in good standing and only a minor number of authorized user accounts, a determination may be made that: (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.253)
- The authorized user accounts had minimal or no impact on the borrower's overall credit profile, and
- The information on the credit report accurately reflects the borrower's credit history

If authorized user tradelines are NOT an accurate reflection of the borrower's credit history, the borrower's credit history must be evaluated without the benefit of those tradelines. (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.253)

### FHLMC — Authorized User Tradeline Requirements

- (Lender note: When LPA returns a feedback message regarding authorized user tradelines, the mortgage file must contain documentation showing for EACH authorized user account that one of the following applies:
  1. Another borrower on the mortgage owns the tradeline, OR
  2. The tradeline is owned by the borrower's spouse, OR
  3. The borrower has been making the payments for the last 12 months AND the monthly payment (as reported on the credit report) is included in the DTI) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.253–254)
- (Lender note: If none of the above can be documented for each authorized user account, a determination may be made that the authorized user accounts have an insignificant impact on the borrower's credit history, based on: number of borrower's own tradelines, age, type, size, and payment history compared to the authorized user accounts. This determination must be documented) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.254)
- (Lender note: If any of the LPA-triggered requirements cannot be met, the LPA feedback certificate is invalid) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.254)

### FHLMC — Revolving and 30-Day Authorized User Accounts (DTI Impact)

- (Lender note: When a borrower is an authorized user on a revolving or 30-day account, the monthly payment as reported on the credit report must be included in DTI ONLY if required documentation shows the borrower has been making the payments for the last 12 months) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.254)

### Additional Considerations (Both GSEs)

- (Lender note: The monthly obligation of authorized user tradelines should only be included in qualifying ratios when: (1) analysis of the tradeline is part of the AUS approval AND (2) the borrower can document 12 months of payments) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.255)

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ The key risk scenario is a borrower who has a thin credit file supplemented by multiple authorized user accounts from a family member. DU may approve based on those tradelines even though they don't reflect the borrower's own credit behavior. UWs should probe this situation when authorized user accounts constitute the majority of a borrower's tradelines.

⚠️ FHLMC's LPA trigger for the authorized user documentation requirement is conditional — the requirement only applies when LPA issues a specific feedback message. If LPA returns Accept without the message, no investigation is required.
