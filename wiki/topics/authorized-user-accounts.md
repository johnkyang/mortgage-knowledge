## Overview

An authorized user is a person permitted by an account owner to use a credit account. Authorized user tradelines establish credit history for the user based on the owner's payment record, even though the user is not the primary account holder. FNMA and FHLMC differ in how they evaluate these tradelines, and DU and manual underwriting also apply different rules. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-06, B3-5.3-09)

---

## Key Rules

### FNMA — Manual Underwriting: Authorized User Tradelines

For manually underwritten loans, authorized user tradelines **cannot** be considered in the underwriting decision, except in the following cases:

An authorized user tradeline **may** be considered if:
- Another borrower in the mortgage transaction is the **owner** of the tradeline, OR
- The borrower provides written documentation (e.g., canceled checks, payment receipts) that they have been the **actual and sole payer** of the monthly payment for at least **12 months** preceding the application date

If the borrower is documented as the actual payer, then:
- The payment history (including late payments) **must** be considered in the credit analysis
- The monthly payment obligation **must** be included in the DTI ratio

An authorized user tradeline **must** be considered if the owner is the **borrower's spouse** and the spouse is not a borrower on the mortgage. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-06)

### FNMA — DU Loan Casefiles: Authorized User Tradelines

DU takes authorized user tradelines into consideration as part of its credit risk assessment. However, the lender must review such tradelines to ensure they accurately reflect the borrower's credit history.

When the borrower has several authorized user accounts but only a few accounts of their own, the lender should establish:
- The relationship of the borrower to the account owner
- Whether the borrower uses the account
- Whether the borrower makes the payments on the account

Lenders are **not required** to review an authorized user tradeline that belongs to the borrower's spouse when the spouse is not on the mortgage transaction.

If authorized user tradelines belong to another borrower on the mortgage, **no additional investigation is needed**.

If the tradelines are not an accurate reflection of the borrower's credit history, the lender should evaluate the borrower's credit history **without** the benefit of those tradelines. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-09)

**DU Guidance on "insignificant impact"**: If the borrower has several tradelines in good standing and only a minor number of authorized user accounts, the lender could determine that:
- The authorized user accounts had minimal or no impact on the overall credit profile, AND
- The credit report accurately reflects the borrower's credit history

(Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-09)

### FHLMC — Authorized User Tradeline Requirements

(Lender note: When LPA returns a feedback message regarding authorized user tradelines, the mortgage file must document for EACH authorized user account that one of the following applies:
1. Another borrower on the mortgage owns the tradeline, OR
2. The tradeline is owned by the borrower's spouse, OR
3. The borrower has been making the payments for the last 12 months AND the monthly payment (as reported on the credit report) is included in DTI

If none of the above can be documented, a determination may be made that the authorized user accounts have an insignificant impact on the borrower's credit history based on: number of the borrower's own tradelines, age, type, size, and payment history compared to the authorized user accounts. This determination must be documented.

If any LPA-triggered requirements cannot be met, the LPA feedback certificate is invalid.) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.253–254)

## Exceptions & Nuances

### DTI Treatment of Authorized User Payments (Manual UW)

For manually underwritten loans, if an authorized user tradeline is documented as being paid by the borrower for 12+ months, the monthly payment **must** be included in DTI. For DU loans, DU handles the DTI calculation based on its own analysis. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-06)

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ The key risk scenario is a borrower with a thin credit file supplemented by multiple authorized user accounts from a family member. DU may issue an Approve based on those tradelines even though they don't reflect the borrower's own credit behavior. UWs should probe this situation when authorized user accounts constitute the majority of a borrower's tradelines.

⚠️ Manual UW and DU have opposite defaults: manual UW **excludes** authorized user tradelines (with exceptions), while DU **includes** them (with lender review). The lender's job under DU is to confirm they're an accurate reflection of the borrower's behavior.

⚠️ FHLMC's LPA trigger for the authorized user documentation requirement is conditional — only applies when LPA issues a specific feedback message. If LPA returns Accept without the message, no investigation is required.

⚠️ Spouse exception: When the spouse is NOT on the loan, the authorized user tradeline belonging to the spouse must be considered in manual UW (if the borrower is an authorized user on the spouse's account), but the lender is NOT required to review it in DU.
