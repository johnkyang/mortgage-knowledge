## Overview

All liabilities affecting income or assets that will impact the borrower's ability to fulfill the mortgage obligation must be considered in the underwriting risk analysis. The mortgage application, credit report, paystubs (if provided), and other file documentation must be reviewed for borrower liabilities. (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.250)

---

## Key Rules

### Obligations Considered Liabilities (Included in DTI)

The following liabilities require monthly payment documentation and must be included in the DTI ratio unless specifically excluded by policy: (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.250)

- Housing payment (mortgage or rent) for each borrower's principal residence
- All revolving charge accounts
- Installment loan debts with a remaining payment term greater than 10 months
- Installment debts secured by virtual currency / cryptocurrency
- Lease payments
- Real estate loans
- Home Equity Lines of Credit (HELOCs)
- Alimony and child support
- Separate maintenance payments
- All other debts of a recurring nature

### Documentation Requirements

- (Lender note: The unpaid balance, terms of repayment, and the borrower's payment history are required for each liability) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.251)
- (Lender note: Verification for any liability not shown on a credit report is required by obtaining documentation from the borrower or creditor) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.251)
- (Lender note: If the credit report does not reference a significant open debt shown on the loan application — including outstanding mortgage debt, bank, student, or credit union loans — a separate credit verification is required) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.251)

### Undisclosed Debt

- (Lender note: If a current liability appears on the credit report that is not on the loan application, the borrower should provide a reasonable explanation; documentation may be required) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.251)
- (Lender note: Recalculation of the DTI ratio is required if additional liabilities are disclosed or discovered after the underwriting decision, up to and concurrent with the Note date) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.251)

### Obligations NOT Considered Liabilities (Excluded from DTI)

The following paystub items and deductions are not considered liabilities and are not included in the DTI: (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.251)

- Federal, state, and local taxes
- FICA or other retirement contributions (e.g., 401(k) accounts), including repayment of debt secured by those funds
- Commuting costs
- Union dues
- Voluntary deductions

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ Installment debts with 10 months or fewer remaining are generally excluded from DTI. See [[total-monthly-debt-obligations]] for the exception where short-term payments significantly affect the borrower's ability to meet credit obligations.

⚠️ Installment debts secured by cryptocurrency/virtual currency are always included in DTI regardless of remaining term — this is an explicit exception to the standard 10-month rule.
