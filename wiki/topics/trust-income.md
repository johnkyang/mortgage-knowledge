## Summary

Trust income may be used as qualifying income when the amount, frequency, and type of income can be verified. The key distinction is whether the trust pays **fixed** or **variable** amounts — this drives both the calculation method and the continuance requirement. A 12-month history of receipt is generally required, with exceptions for trusts with fixed payments where the borrower is not the grantor.

## Key Rules

- Continuance requirement is based on the **type of income received through the trust**, not the trust itself. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
  - Rental income received through a trust → **no continuance documentation required**
  - Fixed payment from a depleting asset → **3-year continuance required**
- Trusts must generally have been established for **12 months or longer** to be considered stable income. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
- A trust established less than 12 months ago may still qualify if **all** of the following are met: (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
  - Trust documentation reflects fixed payments
  - Borrower is **not** the grantor
  - At least **one payment** is received prior to closing
- Trusts created in the prior 12 months using eligible employment-related assets may still qualify, but must meet the income calculation requirements under [[asset-depletion-income]]. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
- **A borrower who is also the trustee may not supply the trustee's statement** — an independent source is required. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## FNMA — Documentation and Calculation

Obtain a copy of one of the following to verify the trust:
- The trust agreement
- The trustee's statement (not acceptable if borrower is the trustee)
- The trust's federal tax returns
- A letter from an accountant or attorney who reviewed the trust documents (when above are unavailable or when the borrower is the trustee)

Confirm: amount, frequency, type of income, and date the trust was established.

Funds from the trust used for down payment, closing costs, or reserves must be **subtracted** from total trust funds before determining if the income meets continuance requirements. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### Fixed Trust Payments (FNMA)
- Use the fixed payment amount from the trust agreement as qualifying income
- Document current receipt with **one month's bank statement** or equivalent
- **3-year continuance is required**
- Payments must have been received for **12 months or longer** to be considered stable
- Exception: trust < 12 months old is acceptable if fixed payments, borrower is not the grantor, and at least one payment received prior to closing

### Variable Trust Payments (FNMA)
- Calculate qualifying income using **variable income and income trending** guidance
- **3-year continuance is required**
- Minimum **24-month history** of trust income required
- Document with borrower's most recent **2 years of federal income tax returns**
- Document current receipt with **1 month's most recent bank statement** or equivalent
(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## FHLMC — Documentation and Calculation

### Variable / Fluctuating Trust Payments (FHLMC)
- History of receipt: **most recent two years**
- Required documents:
  - Copy of fully executed trust agreement outlining payment terms
  - Complete federal individual income tax returns for the most recent **two-year period** verifying receipt of income
  - Evidence of **sufficient assets** to support qualifying income (bank statements, letter from trustee — if borrower is trustee, letter from trustee is not acceptable)
- Calculation: **24-month average**
- Continuance: Document sufficient assets remain after closing to support income for at least **3 more years**

### Fixed / Pre-Determined Trust Payments (FHLMC)
- History of receipt: **most recent one year**
- Required documents:
  - Copy of fully executed trust agreement specifying fixed payment amount, intervals, and duration
  - Bank statements or equivalent verifying receipt of fixed payments for the **most recent one year**
  - Evidence of sufficient assets to support qualifying income (trustee letter not acceptable if borrower is the trustee)
- Calculation: Use the **fixed payment amount** from the trust agreement
- Continuance: Document sufficient assets remain after closing to support income for at least **3 more years**
(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## Exceptions & Nuances

- **Trustee = Borrower**: If the borrower is the trustee, a trustee's statement is not acceptable documentation under either GSE. Require tax returns, trust agreement, or accountant/attorney letter instead.
- **Trust income type drives continuance**: Underwriters sometimes apply a blanket 3-year continuance to all trust income. This is incorrect — if the trust is paying out rental income, no continuance documentation is needed (same rule as direct rental income).
- **Employment-related asset trusts**: A trust funded with employment-related assets (e.g., lump-sum retirement distribution placed in a trust) within the past 12 months must follow [[asset-depletion-income]] rules, not standard trust income rules.

## Common Pitfalls

- Requiring the trustee's statement when the borrower is the trustee — not acceptable; get tax returns or attorney letter instead
- Not subtracting trust assets used for down payment/closing before calculating income continuance
- Applying 3-year continuance to trust income that flows from rental property — not required
- Using a 24-month average for a fixed-payment trust (FHLMC) — fixed payments use the stated trust amount, not an average
- Missing the one-payment-prior-to-closing requirement for trusts less than 12 months old
