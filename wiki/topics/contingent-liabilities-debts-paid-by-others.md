## Overview

Certain liabilities that are technically the borrower's obligation may be excluded from DTI when they have been assigned to another party by court order, or when another party is documented as making the payments. (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.256–258)

---

## Key Rules

### FNMA — Court-Ordered Assignment of Debt

- (Lender note: Court-ordered assignment of debt is NOT included in calculating the borrower's DTI) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.256)
- (Lender note: When a debt has been assigned to another party by court order — such as under a divorce decree or separation agreement — and the creditor does not release the borrower from liability, the borrower has a contingent liability. This contingent liability is not included in DTI) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.256)
- (Lender note: The payment history of the assigned debt after the effective date of the assignment is not considered in the underwriting analysis. The borrower's payment history before assignment is considered per guidelines) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.256)

### FNMA — Debts Paid by Others (Non-Mortgage)

- (Lender note: When a borrower is obligated on a non-mortgage debt but another party is repaying it, the monthly payment may be excluded from DTI, subject to: (1) the other party paying is not required to be obligated on the debt unless they are an interested party to the transaction; AND (2) the most recent 12 months' canceled checks or bank statements from the paying party documenting no delinquencies) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.257)
- (Lender note: Non-mortgage debts include: installment loans, student loans, revolving accounts, lease payments, alimony, child support, and separate maintenance) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.257)

### FNMA — Debts Paid by Others (Mortgage)

- (Lender note: When a borrower is obligated on a mortgage debt but another party is repaying it, the full monthly PITIA may be excluded from DTI, subject to: (1) the paying party IS obligated on the mortgage; (2) most recent 12 months' canceled checks or bank statements from the paying party documenting no delinquencies; AND (3) the borrower is NOT using rental income from that property to qualify) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.257)
- (Lender note: IMPORTANT: Regardless of who makes the mortgage payments, the property must be included in the count of financed properties) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.257)

### FHLMC — Court-Ordered Assignment of Debt

- (Lender note: A liability on a secured or unsecured debt — including a mortgage — may be excluded from DTI if: (1) the debt has been assigned to another by court order such as a divorce decree, AND (2) the court order is documented with the appropriate pages of the agreement) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.257)

### FHLMC — Debt Paid by Others

| Debt Type | Eligibility & Documentation |
|---|---|
| Installment (non-mortgage), Revolving, Monthly lease | Another party (not an interested party*) has been making timely payments for the most recent 12 months |
| Mortgage payment and other property-related expenses (taxes, insurance, HOA) | Another party (obligated on the Note, not an interested party*) has been making timely payments for the most recent 12 months |

*Interested parties include builder, developer, property seller, real estate agent. (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.258)

- (Lender note: The circumstances must be evaluated and validated. For example, a parent paying multiple student loans is common. However, a spouse paying multiple installment and revolving debts for a borrower not on the mortgage requires additional investigation) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.258)

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ The key FNMA distinction: for non-mortgage debt, the paying party does NOT need to be obligated on the debt (anyone can pay). For mortgage debt, the paying party MUST be obligated on the mortgage.

⚠️ The 12-month documentation requirement (canceled checks or bank statements) must come from the paying party, not the borrower. The borrower's bank records showing transfers to pay the obligation are not sufficient.

⚠️ Even when a mortgage debt is excluded from DTI because another party is paying, the property still counts toward the borrower's financed property count — this can affect reserve requirements and LTV eligibility for other financed properties.
