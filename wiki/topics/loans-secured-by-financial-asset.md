## Overview

When a borrower uses a financial asset — such as a life insurance policy, 401(k), IRA, CD, stocks, or bonds — as collateral for a loan, the borrower has a contingent liability. Both GSEs allow this liability to be excluded from DTI under specific conditions. (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.263)

---

## Key Rules

### FNMA — Loan Secured by Financial Asset

- (Lender note: Not required to include this contingent liability in DTI, provided a copy of the loan instrument is obtained that shows the financial asset as collateral) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.263)
- (Lender note: If the borrower intends to use the same asset to satisfy reserve requirements, the value of the asset must be reduced by the loan proceeds and any related fees before determining if reserves are sufficient) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.263)

### FHLMC — Loan Secured by Financial Asset

- (Lender note: Payments on installment debts secured by financial assets — where repayment may be obtained by liquidating the asset — may be excluded from monthly DTI regardless of payment amount or number of payments remaining) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.263)
- (Lender note: The loan secured by the financial asset must have been made by a financial institution) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.263)
- (Lender note: Only the portion of the funds that exceeds the loan balance may be considered as qualifying funds for the borrower) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.263)

### Cryptocurrency/Digital Currency Exception (Both GSEs)

- (Lender note: Installment loans backed by digital currency / cryptocurrency must be included in the borrower's recurring monthly obligations — this exception applies to BOTH FNMA and FHLMC) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.263)

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ The most common scenario is a 401(k) loan. Under FNMA, the 401(k) loan payment is excluded from DTI as long as the loan instrument confirms the 401(k) is the collateral. However, the 401(k) balance must be reduced by the outstanding loan amount when calculating reserves.

⚠️ FHLMC's rule is broader ("regardless of payment amount or number of payments remaining") — this is more favorable than FNMA's because FNMA requires a copy of the instrument and doesn't have an explicit payment/term override.

⚠️ The crypto exclusion is important: even if a loan is technically "secured" by a crypto wallet or digital asset, it does not qualify for the financial asset exemption and must remain in DTI.
