## Overview

When a borrower has an installment agreement with the IRS to repay delinquent income taxes, the monthly payment may be included in DTI in lieu of requiring full payoff — subject to specific conditions. (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.262–263)

---

## Key Rules

### Standard Requirements (Both GSEs)

The company may include the monthly payment (rather than requiring full payoff) if ALL of the following are met: (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.262)
- There is no indication that a notice of federal tax lien has been filed in the county where the subject property is located
- An approved IRS installment agreement with repayment terms — including monthly payment and total amount due — is on file
- Evidence that the borrower is current on the plan, as evidenced by a most recent IRS statement reflecting the last payment amount and next payment date

### FNMA-Specific Requirement

- (Lender note: FNMA: At least one payment must have been made prior to close) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.262)

### 10-Month Remaining Term Exception

- (Lender note: If the IRS installment agreement has fewer than 10 payments remaining, it may be excluded from the borrower's qualifying monthly obligations) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.262)

### Failure to Meet Requirements

- (Lender note: If any of the above requirements cannot be met, the borrower must pay off the outstanding balance due under the agreement) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.262)

### FHLMC — Pending IRS Installment Agreements (Applications dated on or after 9/1/2023)

For IRS installment agreements that are pending approval: (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.262–263)
- Required documentation: The application for the installment agreement reflecting the amount of taxes owed and requested repayment terms
- DTI payment: Use the **greater of**: the monthly payment requested by the borrower per documentation, OR the total taxes owed ÷ 72
- No indication of a filed Notice of Federal Tax Lien may be present

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ The "no federal tax lien" requirement is location-specific — it must be confirmed in the county where the subject property is located. A lien in a different county (where the borrower previously lived) does not necessarily block eligibility, but must be carefully reviewed.

⚠️ FNMA's "at least one payment prior to close" requirement means the installment agreement must already be in repayment at closing — not just established. Pull the IRS statement to confirm the first payment date.

⚠️ FHLMC's ÷72 formula for pending agreements (total owed ÷ 72 months) is a loanDepot/FHLMC standard. This results in a higher qualifying payment than many borrowers expect.
