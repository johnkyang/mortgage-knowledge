## Summary

A large deposit is a single deposit exceeding 50% of total monthly qualifying income (FNMA definition) or 50% of the sum of monthly qualifying income plus any asset-depletion income (FHLMC). When bank statements are used, large deposits must be evaluated. The sourcing requirement depends on the transaction type (purchase vs. refinance) and whether the funds are needed to qualify. Funds from undocumented large deposits must be excluded from available assets.

## Key Rules

### Definition

| GSE | Large Deposit Definition |
|-----|--------------------------|
| FNMA | A single deposit exceeding **50% of total monthly qualifying income** |
| FHLMC | A single deposit exceeding **50% of the sum of** total monthly qualifying income **plus** any asset-depletion income (per FHLMC Section 5307.1), if applicable |

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FNMA — Treatment by Transaction Type

| Transaction Type | Requirement |
|-----------------|-------------|
| Refinance | Documentation or explanation for large deposits is **not required**. However, the underwriter must consider any borrowed funds, including related liabilities, in the evaluation. |
| Purchase | If funds from a large deposit are needed to complete the transaction (down payment, closing costs, or reserves), the source **must be documented** as from an acceptable source. |

**Sourcing approach for FNMA purchases**: When the source cannot be fully documented, reasonable judgment may be used based on available documentation, DTI, income, and credit profile. Acceptable documentation examples:
- Borrower's written explanation
- Proof of ownership of a sold asset
- Copy of wedding invitation (supporting gift receipt)

Written documentation supporting the rationale must be retained in the file. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

**Reduction rule**: Verified funds must be reduced by the **unsourced portion** of the large deposit. Only the remaining (reduced) balance may be used for underwriting.

**Partial sourcing**: When a deposit has both sourced and unsourced portions, only the **unsourced portion** is used to calculate whether it meets the large deposit threshold.

**Example (FNMA)**:
- Monthly income $4,000; deposit of $3,000 with $2,500 documented (tax refund) → unsourced $500 = 12.5% of income → **not** a large deposit → full balance usable.
- Same scenario, only $500 documented → unsourced $2,500 = 63% of income → **large deposit** → $2,500 subtracted from account balance for underwriting.

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

**VOD note**: When using a Verification of Deposit, source of funds is required when the account was opened within 90 days of the application date **and/or** the current balance is significantly higher than the average balance. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FHLMC — Treatment by Transaction Type

| Transaction Type | Requirement |
|-----------------|-------------|
| Refinance | Source of unverified deposits is **not required** to be documented. Any liabilities from borrowed funds must be considered. |
| Purchase | Source of large deposits must be documented if the deposit is **needed to qualify** the borrower (funds required + reserves). |
| Purchase and Refinance | If an unverified deposit was used to **pay off or pay down an existing debt** to qualify, source must be documented **regardless of deposit amount**. |

**FHLMC partial sourcing**: When a deposit has both verified and unverified portions, use only the **unverified amount** when determining whether it's a large deposit.

**FHLMC reduction rule**: If a large deposit is not verified and not needed to qualify, the funds used must be reduced by the amount of the unverified deposit. For LPA mortgages, the reduced asset amount must be entered into LPA.

**Automatic sourcing exception (FHLMC)**: No additional documentation required when the source can be clearly identified from deposit information on the statement itself (e.g., direct payroll deposit) or from other documents in the file (e.g., tax refund amounts matching tax returns). (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FHLMC — Acceptable Sources (When Documentation Required)

- Borrower's income
- Funds awarded to the borrower (disaster relief funds, lottery winnings, court-awarded settlement) — provided the source is not an interested party
- Funds from eligible asset types per Sections 5501.3(b) and 5501.3(c)

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FHLMC — Direct Account Verification (Purchase)

When using a VOD for a purchase transaction, source of funds documentation must be obtained when:
- Account was opened within 90 days of verification, **and/or**
- Current balance exceeds the average balance by more than 50% of the sum of total monthly qualifying income plus any asset-depletion income.

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### AUS Validation

- **FNMA**: When assets are validated through DU Validation Service, DU indicates which large deposits require documentation. Compliance with DU messages satisfies the large deposit documentation requirement.
- **FHLMC**: For LPA mortgages with Risk Class "Accept" and asset representation and warranty result of "Eligible" on the last Feedback Certificate, the asset verification report is acceptable documentation for total funds to be verified on the Feedback Certificate. Any additional funds required must still be verified per normal requirements.

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### Business Assets Exception (FHLMC)

Large deposit documentation is **not required** for business asset accounts, provided the underwriter reviews at least two months of business account statements and determines deposits are typical for the borrower's business. See [[business-assets]]. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
