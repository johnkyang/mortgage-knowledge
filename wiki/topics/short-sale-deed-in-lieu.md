## Overview

Short sales, preforeclosure sales, deeds-in-lieu of foreclosure, and charge-offs of mortgage accounts are alternatives to foreclosure. They carry a 4-year waiting period standard, reduced to 2 years with extenuating circumstances. This is less severe than a standard foreclosure (7 years) but equal to Chapter 7/11 bankruptcy and charge-offs. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-07)

---

## Key Rules

### Definitions

**Deed-in-Lieu of Foreclosure**: A transaction in which the deed to the real property is transferred back to the servicer. Typically identified on credit reports via Remarks Codes such as "Forfeit deed-in-lieu of foreclosure." (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-07)

**Preforeclosure Sale / Short Sale**: Sale of a property in lieu of foreclosure resulting in a payoff of less than the total amount owed, pre-approved by the servicer. Typically identified via Remarks Codes such as "Settled for less than full balance." (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-07)

**Charge-Off of a Mortgage Account**: When a creditor determines there is little or no likelihood of collecting the mortgage debt. Identified with MOP (manner of payment) code "9." Applies to first liens, second liens, home improvement loans, HELOCs, and manufactured home loans. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-09)

### Waiting Periods

| Condition | Waiting Period |
|---|---|
| Standard | **4 years** from completion date |
| Extenuating circumstances documented | **2 years** from completion date |

The completion date is as reported on the credit report or other borrower-provided documents. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-07)

**Note**: Unlike foreclosure, there are NO additional LTV or occupancy restrictions during the standard waiting period. Once the 4-year period (or 2-year EC period) has elapsed, the borrower is eligible without further restrictions beyond standard program eligibility. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-07)

### Reporting Inconsistencies

Deeds-in-lieu and preforeclosure sales may not be accurately or consistently reported across creditors and credit reporting agencies. If not clearly identified in the credit report, lenders must obtain appropriate documentation establishing the completion date of the event. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-07)

### Re-establishing Credit After These Events

After a deed-in-lieu, short sale, or mortgage charge-off, credit is considered re-established when ALL of the following are met:

1. The applicable waiting period and additional requirements are met
2. The loan receives a DU Approve/Eligible recommendation or, if manually underwritten, meets minimum credit score requirements
3. The borrower has **traditional credit** — nontraditional credit or "thin files" are not acceptable

(Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-07)

### DU Handling of These Events

**Deed-in-Lieu (DIL):**
DU identifies DILs using specific Remarks Codes. When identified, the lender must document: completed ≥4 years from disbursement, or ≥2 years from disbursement if EC applies and the mortgage meets all EC requirements.

**Preforeclosure Sale (PFS) / Short Sale:**
DU identifies PFS using specific Remarks Codes. Same documentation requirements as DIL.

**Mortgage Charge-Off:**
DU identifies charge-offs via MOP code "9," when no foreclosure indicators (MOP "8," foreclosure Remarks Code) or DIL/PFS Remarks Codes are also present. Same documentation requirements.

(Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-09)

## Exceptions & Nuances

### Timeshare Accounts

Timeshare accounts are considered installment loans. Timeshare deed-in-lieu or charge-offs are not subject to these waiting periods. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-07)

### High LTV Refinance Loans

Significant derogatory credit event waiting periods do not apply to high LTV refinance loans. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-07)

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ Short sales and preforeclosure sales are used interchangeably in the Selling Guide and have the same meaning and the same waiting periods.

⚠️ The key advantage of a short sale over a foreclosure is the waiting period difference: 4 years vs. 7 years (standard). Borrowers who have a choice between pursuing a foreclosure or short sale should be aware of this material difference in future mortgage eligibility.

⚠️ Charge-offs of mortgage accounts are treated the same as deeds-in-lieu and short sales (4-year/2-year EC), NOT as simple non-mortgage charge-offs. The borrower does not need to pay off the charged-off mortgage balance before applying for a new loan (after the waiting period), but the event still counts as a significant derogatory credit event.
