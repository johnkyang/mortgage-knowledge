## Overview

Real estate taxes must be calculated based on no less than the current assessed value. However, certain discounts, exemptions, and abatements may be used to reduce the qualifying property tax amount under specific conditions. (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.260–262)

---

## Key Rules

### Base Tax Calculation

- (Lender note: The calculation of real estate taxes must be based on no less than the current assessed value of the collateral. Property taxes are listed on the title commitment or tax certification from the local taxing authority) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.260)
- (Lender note: Taxes cannot be reduced if a tax reduction appeal is pending or has not been approved) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.260)
- (Lender note: For new construction where the actual tax amount is not yet available, a reasonable estimate based on the value of the land plus completed improvements must be used) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.260)

### Discounted Property Taxes

- (Lender note: When discounted property taxes are used for borrower qualifying, impounds for property taxes are mandatory to ensure taxes are paid within the period required to receive the discount) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.431)
- (Lender note: Use of discounted property taxes varies based on product type) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.431)

**Example (Florida):** Tax bills issued in November; due 3/31 of following year. Discount schedule: 4% if paid by 11/30 → 3% if paid by 12/31 → 2% if paid by 1/31 → 1% if paid by 2/28 → 0% if paid after 2/28 but before 4/1. Tax escrow accounts are scheduled for payment in November using the applicable discount rate. (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.260)

### Property Tax Exemptions

- (Lender note: Property Tax Exemptions are savings that reduce a homeowner's tax bill. Types include homestead, senior citizen, disabled veteran, etc. Requirements vary by location) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.261)
- (Lender note: The Homestead Exemption is the most common. All states have it except New Jersey and Pennsylvania) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.261)
- (Lender note: Exemptions are NOT automatic — homeowners must apply for and be approved by their local taxing authority) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.261)
- (Lender note: For ability-to-repay and cash-to-close purposes, only a homestead or disabled veteran exemption may be taken into consideration PRIOR to the exemption being approved) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.261)
- (Lender note: FHLMC: Exemptions and/or abatements may reduce the subject property tax for qualification when the reduction is documented and continuance requirements [5 years] are met. Exemptions associated with age or disability do not require continuance evidence if there is no pre-determined expiration date within 5 years of the Note date) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.261)

### Tax Abatements

- (Lender note: A tax abatement is a tax break offered by a state or municipality on certain real estate types, typically to encourage development or economic activity) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.261)
- (Lender note: A tax abatement must be scheduled to last for no less than 5 years from the Note date for the borrower to qualify with the reduced tax amount) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.261)
  - Municipality with 10-year abatement → borrower may qualify with reduced tax amount
  - Municipality with 10-year abatement with annual increases in years 1–10 → borrower must qualify with the annual taxes required at the end of year 5 after the first payment date

(Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.261)

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ The homestead/disabled veteran exemption carve-out (allowed before approval) is specific — other exemption types (senior, etc.) cannot be applied to reduce qualifying taxes until officially approved.

⚠️ FHLMC's 5-year continuance rule for abatements/exemptions has an exception for age/disability exemptions with no expiration date. This matters in markets like Florida where senior exemptions are permanent once granted.

⚠️ For refi transactions, a tax abatement may appear on title or the tax bill — underwriters should confirm the remaining abatement term (must be ≥5 years from Note date) before using the reduced amount.
