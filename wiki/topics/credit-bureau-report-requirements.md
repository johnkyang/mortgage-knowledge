## Overview

A credit report must be obtained for each borrower who has an individual credit record. The report must be based on data from the three national credit repositories. (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.313–314)

---

## Key Rules

### Report Requirements

- (Lender note: A credit report is required for each borrower on the loan application who has an individual credit record) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.313)
- (Lender note: The credit report must be based on data provided by the national credit repositories) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.313)
- (Lender note: All loans require a tri-merge credit report that identifies a credit score for each borrower) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.314)
- (Lender note: The report must generate an inquiry that will appear on future credit reports and must list all inquiries made in the previous 90 days) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.314)

### Accepted Credit Repositories

The three national credit repositories accepted by loanDepot are: (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.313)
- Equifax Credit Information Services (BEACON Score)
- Experian Information Systems and Services (Fair Isaac Credit Score)
- TransUnion Credit Information Company (Empirica Score)

### FICO Scoring Models

- (Lender note: Each credit bureau scores the applicant using the Fair Isaac Company (FICO) model. BEACON, Empirica, and Fair Isaac Credit Scores are all acceptable to loanDepot and are collectively referred to as "the credit score") (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.314)

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ The tri-merge requirement (all three repositories) is standard lender policy. See [[frozen-credit-bureaus]] for how this interacts with situations where one or two bureaus have frozen credit.

⚠️ "Individual credit record" is not explicitly defined in this section. Borrowers with no credit score are covered under a separate policy — see [[no-credit-score-borrowers]].
