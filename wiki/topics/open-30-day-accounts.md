## Overview

Open 30-day charge accounts require the full balance to be paid each month. They are indicated by no monthly payment reporting on the credit report, or a payment equal to the outstanding balance. Their DTI treatment differs from revolving accounts. (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.267–268)

---

## Key Rules

### FNMA — 30-Day Account DTI and Fund Verification

- (Lender note: Open 30-day charge account balances are NOT included in the DTI ratio) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.267)
- (Lender note: For 30-day accounts that do not reflect a monthly payment, OR reflect a payment identical to the account balance: verification of borrower funds to cover the account balance is required) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.267)
- (Lender note: Funds sufficient to cover the balance must be IN ADDITION TO any funds used for closing costs and reserves, and must come from an eligible source) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.267)
- (Lender note: If the borrower paid off — or will pay off — the account balance prior to closing, proof of payoff in lieu of verifying funds is required. Verification of the source of payoff funds is also required) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.267)
- (Lender note: DU will include 30-day charge account balances in the "Reserves Required to be Verified" amount on the DU Findings report. For transactions not requiring reserve verification, the 30-day balance is reduced by any cash-out the borrower receives through the transaction) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.267)

### FHLMC — 30-Day Account DTI and Fund Verification

- (Lender note: Open 30-day charge account balances may NOT be included in DTI if sufficient borrower funds to pay off the full outstanding balance are documented) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.267)
- (Lender note: If sufficient funds cannot be verified, the full amount of the outstanding balance must be included in DTI) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.267–268)
- (Lender note: Funds sufficient to cover the balance must be in addition to any funds used for closing costs and reserves, and must come from an eligible source) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.268)

### Hybrid 30-Day / Revolving Accounts

- (Lender note: Some 30-day accounts are structured with BOTH a pay-in-full portion (Open 30-Day Account) AND a pay-over-time portion (Revolving). The pay-in-full portion must be paid in full every month; the revolving portion has a monthly payment that must be included in DTI) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.268)

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ American Express charge cards and corporate travel cards are the most common 30-day accounts. These frequently have large outstanding balances that create asset verification requirements even though they don't appear in DTI.

⚠️ FNMA treats this as a reserve/asset issue (funds must exist) rather than a DTI issue (balance not in ratio). FHLMC treats it as a conditional DTI issue (balance out of DTI only if funds are verified). The practical outcome is similar, but the mechanism differs.

⚠️ The hybrid card scenario (part charge, part revolving) — common with some Amex products — requires careful parsing: the charge portion follows 30-day rules, the revolving portion follows revolving rules.
