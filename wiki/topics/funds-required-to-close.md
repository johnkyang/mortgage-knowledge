## Summary

Funds required to close (cash to close) is the total amount of money required to complete the mortgage transaction — including down payment, closing costs, prepaids, and reserves. All funds must come from an eligible source. AUS (DU/LPA) determines which assets must be verified and in what amount.

## Key Rules

### FNMA — DU Findings Components

DU identifies the following values related to funds to close:
- **Total Available Assets**: Sum of all borrowers' liquid assets entered in DU.
- **Funds Required to Close**: Cash needed to complete the transaction, plus any debts marked "paid by closing" (excluding subject property mortgages) on purchase or limited cash-out refinance transactions.
- **Reserves Required to be Verified**: Amount of reserves that must be verified.
- **Total Funds to be Verified**: Funds Required to Close + Reserves Required to be Verified.
- **Excess Available Assets**: Not required to be verified by DU.

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

- DU indicates the **minimum verification documentation** required; the underwriter must determine if additional documentation is warranted for the borrower's specific situation. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
- DU will **not require** asset documentation for refinance transactions when total funds to be verified are **$500 or less**. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FHLMC

- Borrowers must have sufficient funds to qualify unless **all** of the following apply:
  - The transaction is a refinance, **and**
  - Funds required to be paid by the borrower are **$500 or less**, **and**
  - No reserves are required for the transaction, **and**
  - The mortgage is an LPA mortgage with a risk class of **Accept**.
- All funds used to qualify the borrower — including down payment, closing costs, and reserves — must come from an eligible source.
- Prorated real estate tax credits contributed by the property seller (in areas where taxes are paid in arrears) may **not** be considered when determining funds required. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
