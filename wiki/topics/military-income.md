## Overview

Military personnel may receive multiple types of pay beyond base pay. These can be used as qualifying income provided the underwriter can establish the income will continue to be received in the future. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## Key Rules

### Eligible Military Income Types
The following are acceptable stable income sources (continuance must be establishable):
- Flight or hazard duty pay
- Rations — **Basic Allowance for Subsistence (BAS)**
- Clothing allowance
- Quarters' allowance — **Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH)**
- Proficiency pay

> Note: BAS and BAH are nontaxable income — gross-up rules may apply. See [[social-security-retirement-income]] for gross-up guidance.

### Military Reserve / National Guard Income
Income paid to military reservists while satisfying reserve obligations is acceptable **if it meets the same stability and continuity tests applied to secondary employment**.

---

### FNMA-Specific Rules
- Verify military base pay and entitlements with the borrower's **most recent Leave and Earnings Statement (LES)**.
- Military income and employment are **not eligible for DU validation service**.

### FHLMC-Specific Rules

| Borrower Type | Employment History Required? |
|---|---|
| Active-duty military | **Not required** — no history needed for stability |
| Military reserve / National Guard | **1-year history** of receipt required |

**Required documentation** (all of the following):
- YTD military LES, **and**
- W-2 for the most recent calendar year (military LES may substitute if it reflects complete prior-year income)

**OR** all of the following:
- Written VOE documenting all YTD earnings and earnings for the most recent calendar year, **and**
- 10-day Pre-Closing Verification (PCV)

**AIM (Asset and Income Modeler) automated income assessment with LPA is not eligible for military income.**

## Exceptions & Nuances

- Reserve/National Guard income requires the same analysis as secondary employment — evaluate for stability and continuity before using.
- BAH and BAS are nontaxable; if used for qualifying, the 25% gross-up factor applies (see Nontaxable Income policy).
