## Summary

Assets are anything of value used for funds to close, down payment, and/or reserves. Eligible asset types must be verified through an approved method — Verification of Deposit (VOD), bank/investment statements, or third-party digital verification. FNMA and FHLMC have parallel but distinct documentation standards. AUS validation (DU/LPA) may satisfy verification requirements when flagged on the findings report.

## Key Rules

### Eligible Asset Types

The following are eligible asset types for funds to close, down payment, and/or reserves:
- Depository accounts (checking, savings, money market, CDs)
- Securities (stocks, vested stock options, bonds, mutual funds, U.S. government securities)
- Retirement accounts (401k, 403b, IRA — traditional and Roth, SEP-IRA, SIMPLE-IRA, KEOGH, state retirement plans)
- Gift funds (from eligible donors — see [[gift-funds]])
- Gift of equity (see [[gift-of-equity]])
- Proceeds from a loan fully secured by the borrower's assets (non-real property)
- Proceeds from the sale or refinance of real property (including 1031 exchange)
- Proceeds from the sale of the borrower's assets (non-real property, non-exchange-traded securities)
- Borrower's earned real estate commission
- Funds from a trust

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### AUS Validation

When assets are validated by the AUS (DU or LPA), compliance with AUS messages satisfies the documentation requirement. No additional verification is required for those assets. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FNMA — Acceptable Verification Methods

1. **Verification of Deposit (VOD)**: Must be requested directly from the depository institution; must be complete, signed, and dated; returned directly from the institution.
2. **Bank / investment portfolio statements**: Must clearly identify the financial institution, borrower as account holder, last four digits of account number, time period, all deposits/withdrawals (depository) or purchase/sale transactions (investment), and ending balance.
3. **Third-party asset verification vendor**: Acceptable if borrower provided proper authorization; information conforms to what would be on a VOD or bank statement; date of completed verification is within age-of-document policy.
4. **Retirement account statements**: Most recent statement; must identify borrower's vested amount and withdrawal terms.

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FNMA — Statement Periods Required

| Transaction Type | Statement Period |
|-----------------|-----------------|
| Purchase | Most recent full 2-month period (60 days; or most recent quarter if reported quarterly) |
| Refinance | Most recent full 1-month period (30 days; or most recent quarter if reported quarterly) |

If the latest bank statement is more than 45 days before the loan application date, a supplemental bank-generated document showing the last four digits, balance, and date is required. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FHLMC — Direct Account Verification (VOD) Requirements

VODs must identify: financial institution, account owners, account number (minimum last four digits), account type, account open date, current balance, average balance for prior two months, any outstanding loans secured by the asset, and include the depository representative's title, signature, and phone number.

**FHLMC Purchase transaction note**: When using a VOD, source of funds must be documented when an account is opened within 90 days of verification **and/or** when the current balance exceeds the average balance by more than 50% of the sum of total monthly qualifying income plus any asset-depletion income amount. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FHLMC — Asset Account Statement Requirements

Must identify: financial institution, account owners, account number (minimum last four digits), all transactions, period covered, ending balance, and any outstanding loans secured by the asset. Computer-generated or internet-downloaded transaction histories are acceptable if they identify the institution and source. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FHLMC — Third-Party Verification Service

Third-party asset verification is acceptable if received directly from the provider and contains required information. Exceptions for electronically-generated (non-rep-completed) verifications: representative information not required; minimum last two digits of account number acceptable. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FHLMC — Age of Verification

Verification of source of funds must be dated no more than 120 days before the Note date (or Effective Date of Permanent Financing for renovation mortgages). Verifications completed after the Note date do not satisfy the requirement. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## Exceptions & Nuances

- Portal screenshots or photos of documentation are acceptable for FHLMC as long as guideline and delivery requirements are met.
- Internet-downloaded statements must clearly identify the financial institution name and URL/fax banner source.
- For assets not meeting standard age requirements, supplemental documentation is required (FNMA: within 45 days; FHLMC: within 120 days).
