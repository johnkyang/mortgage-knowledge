## Overview

When a borrower has frozen credit at one or more national repositories, the loan may still be eligible under specific conditions. Rules differ between FNMA and FHLMC. (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.322)

---

## Key Rules

### FNMA — Frozen Credit Requirements

- (Lender note: If the borrower's credit is frozen at ONE of the three repositories, the credit report is still acceptable if: (1) credit data is available from two repositories, (2) a credit score is obtained from at least one of those two repositories, and (3) a three in-file merged report was requested) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.322)
- (Lender note: Loans for borrowers with credit data frozen at TWO OR MORE of the credit repositories are NOT eligible) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.322)

### FHLMC — Frozen Credit Requirements

- (Lender note: For borrowers with frozen credit, no more than one of the national credit repositories can have frozen credit information) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.422)

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ Both FNMA and FHLMC allow up to one frozen bureau — the practical rule is the same. The key difference is that FNMA requires a credit score from at least one of the two unfrozen repositories; FHLMC does not explicitly state this requirement in the loanDepot guide but implies it by allowing one freeze.

⚠️ When only two scores are available due to a frozen bureau, the per-borrower score selection method uses the lower of the two available scores. See [[credit-score-selection]] for the full selection logic.

⚠️ Borrowers with no credit score (all bureaus frozen or no credit file) are handled under a separate policy. See [[no-credit-score-borrowers]].
