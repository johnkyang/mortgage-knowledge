## Summary

Eligible employment-related assets or retirement assets can be used as a source of qualifying income when the borrower lacks sufficient employment income. The calculation method and eligibility requirements differ significantly between FNMA and FHLMC. FNMA divides net documented assets by the loan term in months; FHLMC divides by 240 months regardless of loan term and applies a 62-year-old age requirement for depository/securities accounts.

## Key Rules

### General
- Assets used to calculate income must be **owned individually** by the borrower, or the co-owner must be a co-borrower. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
- Assets must be **liquid and available** to the borrower. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
- **Cryptocurrency income is not eligible** as qualifying income. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
- Asset depletion income requires **3-year continuance documentation** (distributions from retirement accounts are on the defined-expiration list). (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### Eligible Asset Types — FNMA
1. **Non-self-employed severance or lump-sum retirement package**: Must be documented with employer distribution letter (Form 1099-R) and deposited to a verified asset account
2. **401(k), IRA, SEP, or Keogh retirement accounts**: Borrower must have unrestricted access (unqualified and unlimited right to request full distribution); if penalty applies, subtract the penalty amount first
3. Checking and savings accounts are generally **not eligible** unless the balance can be traced to an eligible employment-related source (e.g., severance package, lump-sum retirement distribution)

### Ineligible Assets — FNMA
- Stock options
- Non-vested restricted stock
- Lawsuits
- Lottery winnings
- Sale of real estate
- Inheritance and divorce proceeds
(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## FNMA — Net Documented Assets Calculation

**Step 1**: Start with total eligible assets
**Step 2**: Subtract early distribution penalty (if applicable — typically 10%)
**Step 3**: Subtract funds needed for down payment, closing costs, and required reserves
**Result** = Net Documented Assets
**Monthly income** = Net Documented Assets ÷ loan term in months (e.g., 360 for a 30-year loan)

**Example:**
- IRA balance: $500,000
- Less 10% early distribution penalty: –$50,000
- Subtotal: $450,000
- Less funds required for closing: –$100,000
- Net Documented Assets: $350,000
- Monthly income: $350,000 ÷ 360 = **$972.22/month**
(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## FHLMC — Asset Eligibility and Calculation

**Eligible Asset Types:**

| Asset Type | Key Requirements |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Retirement assets (401k, IRA, etc.) | Borrower must be sole owner; not currently used as income; as of Note date, must have full withdrawal access without penalty or early distribution tax |
| Lump-sum distributions (in retirement account) | Follow retirement asset requirements |
| Lump-sum distributions (in depository/securities) | Must derive from IRS-recognized retirement account; borrower must have been recipient; immediately accessible; not subject to penalty or early distribution tax |
| Depository accounts and securities | Borrower must be sole owner (or all joint owners are co-borrowers); **at least one borrower must be 62+ years old**; immediately accessible without penalty; held in US-regulated institution in USD |
| Assets from sale of business | Borrower must be sole owner of proceeds; immediately accessible; sale must not have resulted in retained business assets, existing debt, or seller-held notes |

**Cryptocurrency**: May NOT be included in FHLMC asset depletion calculation (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

**FHLMC Calculation:**
- Monthly income = Net Eligible Assets ÷ **240 months** (regardless of actual loan term)
- Net Eligible Assets = Total eligible assets minus: funds required for the transaction (down payment, closing costs) + gift funds + borrowed funds + pledged/encumbered assets

**FHLMC Loan Parameters:**
- Property: 1- or 2-unit primary residence or second home
- Loan purpose: Purchase or no cash-out refinance
- Maximum LTV: 70% (or 80% if at least one owner of the assets is 62+ years old at closing)
(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

**FHLMC — Sourcing deposits in depository/securities accounts:**
- Must document source of any deposit exceeding **10% of total eligible assets** in depository/securities accounts
- Must verify deposit does not include gifts or borrowed funds
- OR reduce eligible assets by the deposit amount
- Exception: Clearly identifiable deposits (payroll direct deposits) do not require additional documentation
(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

**LDW (Direct channel) Note:** FHLMC lump-sum distributions not in a retirement account, depository/securities accounts, and assets from the sale of a business are NOT permitted to be used as income. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## Exceptions & Nuances

- **FHLMC 62-year-old requirement**: Applies only to depository accounts and securities — not to retirement accounts (which have their own eligibility criteria without the age requirement)
- **FNMA vs. FHLMC income stream formula**: FNMA uses actual loan term (typically 360 months); FHLMC uses 240 months — this means FHLMC yields higher monthly income from the same asset balance
- **Loans where asset depletion is not the only option**: If the loan doesn't meet FHLMC parameters, assets may still qualify under Interest and Dividends Income or Retirement Income guidelines
- **Investor Feature Identifier**: FNMA loans using employment-related assets as income require IFI code H31

## Common Pitfalls

- Including cryptocurrency in the asset balance — ineligible for both FNMA and FHLMC
- Not subtracting the early distribution penalty before dividing (FNMA) — the 10% penalty is often overlooked on pre-age-59½ distributions
- Forgetting to subtract funds needed for closing before calculating the income stream
- Applying FHLMC's 240-month divisor to an FNMA loan (or vice versa)
- Missing the FHLMC age requirement (62+) for depository/securities accounts
- Attempting to use checking/savings as asset depletion income (FNMA) when the source isn't traceable to an eligible employment-related asset
