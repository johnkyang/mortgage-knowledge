## Summary

Vested funds from IRA, SEP/KEOGH, 401(k), 403(b), and other IRS-qualified retirement accounts are acceptable sources for down payment, closing costs, and reserves. The underwriter must confirm the borrower has reasonable access to the funds. Retirement accounts that restrict withdrawals or require employment termination/severance are ineligible. When retirement account funds are used for reserves, they do not need to be withdrawn.

## Key Rules

### Eligible Retirement Account Types

- Individual retirement accounts: IRA (traditional and Roth), SEP-IRA, SIMPLE-IRA, KEOGH
- IRS-qualified employer retirement accounts: 401(k), 403(b), state retirement plans, and other IRS-qualified employer plan accounts

**Ineligible**: Accounts restricting the borrower's ability to make withdrawals, or requiring severance from current employment, are ineligible (see [[ineligible-assets]]). (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FNMA — Documentation Requirements

The most recent monthly or quarterly statement must be reviewed to verify:
- Existence and amounts in the borrower's retirement accounts
- Borrower's eligibility for withdrawals
- Terms and conditions for withdrawal

The ownership of the account must be verified and confirmed to ensure the account is vested and allows withdrawals regardless of the borrower's current employment status. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

**If retirement assets are in stocks, bonds, or mutual funds**: The account must also meet FNMA requirements for [[stocks-bonds-mutual-funds]] to determine value and whether liquidation evidence is required for down payment/closing cost use. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

**Reserves**: Retirement account funds used for reserves do **not** need to be withdrawn from the accounts. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FHLMC — Documentation Requirements

The underwriter must confirm reasonable access to funds by reviewing acceptable documentation verifying existence, amounts, withdrawal eligibility, and withdrawal terms.

| Documentation Level | Statement Period |
|--------------------|-----------------|
| Streamlined Accept | Account statement covering 1-month period **or** direct account verification (VOD) |
| Standard | Account statement covering 2-month period **or** direct account verification (VOD) |

When evidence of liquidation is **not** obtained, to use a vested IRS-qualified employer retirement account for qualification, the mortgage file must include documentation confirming:
- The borrower is permitted to make withdrawals, **and**
- Severance from current employment is **not** required to make withdrawals.

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FHLMC — Liquidation Evidence Requirement

When retirement account assets are needed for closing (as part of a group including stocks, bonds, mutual funds, U.S. government securities, or other securities), evidence of liquidation is required **unless** the combined value of all those assets is at least **20% greater** than the amount needed for closing.

This 20% buffer test applies across all securities and retirement accounts combined (not per account). (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
