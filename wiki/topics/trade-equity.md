## Summary

Trade equity is the net proceeds remaining after a borrower sells or trades-in a previously owned property to finance the down payment on the purchase of a new property. It is an eligible source of funds to supplement the minimum borrower contribution. The equity contribution is calculated as the lesser of appraised value or trade-in value, minus outstanding liens and transfer costs.

## Key Rules

### FNMA

Trade equity is an acceptable source of funds to **supplement** the minimum borrower contribution, provided:
- The seller's equity contribution for the traded property is a **true-value consideration**, supported by a current appraisal.
- Borrowers must still make the minimum required contribution from their own funds **unless**:
  - The LTV or CLTV ratio is **≤ 80%**, **or**
  - The borrowers are purchasing a **one-unit principal residence** and meet the requirements to use gifts, donated grant funds, or employer funds to pay for some or all of the minimum contribution.

These requirements apply to all property trade transactions, including those structured as two separate contracts with buyer/seller roles reversed on the second contract. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

**DU entry**: Trade equity is entered in the loan application as a **credit to the transaction** and reduces the borrower's required funds to close. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FNMA — Calculating the Equity Contribution

Equity contribution = **(Lesser of appraised value or trade-in value)** minus outstanding mortgage balance of the traded property minus transfer costs. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FNMA — Documentation Requirements

- Transfer deed must be recorded.
- Search of land records to verify ownership and determine existing liens.
- Proof of title transfer and satisfaction of any existing mortgage liens the borrower was liable for.

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FHLMC

The borrower's equity in the previously-owned residence is determined by subtracting outstanding liens and transfer costs from the lesser of the **appraised value** or its **trade-in value as shown in the trade-in contract**.

**Documentation required**:
- Appraisal of the borrower's previously-owned residence.
- Copy of the trade-in contract.

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## Exceptions & Nuances

- Refer to Manufactured Homes section for trade equity from a borrower's existing manufactured home — different rules may apply.
- Trade equity reduces funds to close but does not provide liquid assets that count as reserves.
