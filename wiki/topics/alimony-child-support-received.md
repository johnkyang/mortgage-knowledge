## Summary

Alimony, child support, equalization payments, and separate maintenance income may be used as qualifying income when the obligation is documented, payments have a demonstrated 6-month history of full, regular, and timely receipt, and at least 3-year continuance is established. The borrower must disclose the income and request it be considered. Child support is nontaxable and may be grossed up.

## Key Rules — FNMA (B3-3.4-02)

- Income may be considered **only if the borrower discloses it on the URLA and requests it be considered** in qualifying. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])
- **Lump sum equalization payments** are not considered a steady source of income. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])
- **Proposed or voluntary payments** (no separation agreement specifying amounts) may not be used as income. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])
- If separated but no separation agreement specifying alimony/child support, the lender should not consider any proposed or voluntary payments as income. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])
- **Child support is nontaxable** and may be grossed up per B3-3.1-01 nontaxable income guidelines. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])
- **DU Validation Service**: If validated by DU, follow DU message documentation requirements — may differ from standard. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])

## Income History

- **Minimum 6-month history** is required. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])
- Payment history must demonstrate stability: full, regular, and timely payments. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])

## Income Continuance

- **Minimum 3-year continuance** from the note date must be documented. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])
- **Child support**: The ages of the children for whom support is paid must support at least 3 years of continued payments from the application date. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])

## Documentation Requirements

**Payment terms** — obtain one of the following:
- Copy of divorce decree or separation agreement (if divorce is not final, separation agreement is acceptable)
- Any other written legal agreement or court decree describing payment terms
- Documentation verifying applicable state law that mandates such payments

**Receipt history** — document for the most recent 6 months using:
- Bank statements
- Cancelled checks
- Evidence of other electronic receipt of payments

(Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])

### loanDepot overlay (same requirements, confirmed consistent):
- Legally binding copy of divorce decree, separation agreement, or similar court order
- Proof of receipt of regular full payments for the most recent 6 months
(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## Calculation

- The monthly payment amount as documented may be used as qualifying income. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])
- Child support income is **nontaxable** and may be grossed up (see B3-3.1-01 for gross-up factor). (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])

## Common Pitfalls

- Using proposed or voluntary payments where no legal obligation exists
- Failing to confirm child support ages support 3-year continuance (a 10-year-old with payments ending at 18 = only 8 years remaining — no issue; a 16-year-old may fall short)
- Treating lump sum equalization payments as income — explicitly ineligible
- Not grossing up child support — it is nontaxable income and may be grossed up for a qualifying income benefit
- Accepting income the borrower did not disclose or did not request to be considered
