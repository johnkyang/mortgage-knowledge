## Overview

Alimony, child support, and separate maintenance payments may be used as qualifying income when the obligation is documented and payments have a demonstrated history and sufficient continuance. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## Key Rules

### Eligibility Requirements
Income may be used to qualify if **both** conditions are met:
1. The payer was **obligated** to make payments for the **most recent 6 months**
2. The payer is **obligated** to continue payments for the **next 3 years**

### Stability Standard
- Payments must be **full, regular, and timely** for **6 months or longer** to be considered stable income.
- Income received for **less than 6 months** is considered **unstable** and may **not** be used to qualify.
- Payments made on an **inconsistent or sporadic basis** — whether full or partial — are **not acceptable** for qualifying purposes.

### Child Support — Age of Children
For child support, the **ages of the children must support a minimum of 3 years of continued payments** from the application date.

### Required Documentation
1. A **legally binding copy** of the divorce decree, separation agreement, or similar court order verifying:
   - The amount of payments
   - The duration of payments (must show minimum 3-year continuance from application)
2. **Proof of receipt** of the regular full payments for the **most recent 6 months**
