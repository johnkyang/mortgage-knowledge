## Summary

Base income is received regularly independent of bonuses or overtime. FNMA distinguishes between two types: **fixed base** (set salary or guaranteed hours) and **variable base** (fluctuating hours or varying hourly rate). Fixed base requires no minimum history; variable base requires 12 months and uses trending analysis to calculate qualifying income.

## Key Rules

### Fixed Base Income (B3-3.3-01)
- **Definition**: A set salary or fixed hourly rate with guaranteed minimum hours. Minor pay-period variances in hours do not disqualify income from being treated as fixed. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])
- **No minimum history** is required for inclusion as qualifying income. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])
- **No continuance verification** required unless the lender has reason to believe income may not continue. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])

### Variable Base Income (B3-3.3-01)
- **Definition**: A fixed hourly rate with fluctuating hours, or an hourly rate that varies. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])
- **Minimum 12-month history** of receiving variable income is required. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])
- **No continuance verification** required unless the lender has reason to believe income may not continue. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])
- **Pay raises for variable income** must be in place **prior to closing** — the standard pay-raise policy in B3-3.2-02 does not apply to variable income. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])

## Calculation — Fixed Base Income

| How Often Paid | How to Determine Monthly Income |
|---|---|
| Annually | Annual gross pay ÷ 12 months |
| Monthly | Use monthly gross payment amount directly |
| Twice Monthly | Twice monthly gross pay × 2 pay periods |
| Biweekly | (Biweekly gross pay × 26 pay periods) ÷ 12 months |
| Weekly | (Weekly gross pay × 52 pay periods) ÷ 12 months |
| Hourly | (Hourly gross pay × average hours/week × 52 weeks) ÷ 12 months |

**YTD consistency check**: Qualifying income must be consistent with YTD base earnings. If YTD income does not reflect at least 30 days of earnings, the lender must confirm qualifying income is consistent with prior year's earnings. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])

## Calculation — Variable Base Income

Two acceptable methods (lender's choice):

**Method 1 — Average Income (Trending)**
- **Stable or Increasing**: Average of YTD income and prior year earnings. Calculation must include a minimum of 12 months' income.
- **Decreasing**: Must confirm the current income level has stabilized after the decline; otherwise income is ineligible. Calculate as YTD income ÷ months elapsed in the current year.

**Method 2 — Average Hours**
- Multiply average monthly hours (based on at least the most recent 12 months) by the current fixed hourly rate.

**Non-recurring event exclusion**: When a documented event outside the borrower's control temporarily prevented income from being earned, the lender may exclude that period from the calculation. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])

## Documentation Requirements

For both fixed and variable base income, the lender must obtain one of the following:
- A completed Request for Verification of Employment (Form 1005), **or**
- The most recent paystub **and** most recent W-2

Additionally, a **verbal VOE** is required. See [[verbal-voe-requirements]] for specific requirements. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]])

## Common Pitfalls

- Treating variable base income as fixed when hours actually fluctuate — must apply trending analysis
- Applying pay-raise credit to variable income before closing — variable income raises must already be in place
- Using a straight average for variable income when a declining trend exists — must confirm stabilization first
- Not confirming the YTD income reflects at least 30 days when YTD is less than a full month at time of application
