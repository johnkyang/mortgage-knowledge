## Summary

For all qualifying income, the underwriter must determine whether the income is reasonably expected to continue. Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac each define which income types require documented 3-year continuance and which are presumed to continue without documentation. When income has a defined expiration date or depletes an asset, underwriters must consider the borrower's ability to repay after the income ends.

## FNMA — Continuance Framework

### No Continuance Documentation Required
These income types are presumed indefinite — do NOT document 3-year continuance:
- Base salary / hourly
- Bonus, overtime, commission, or tip income
- Automobile allowance
- Capital gains income
- Corporate retirement or pension
- Long-term disability income
- Foster-care income
- Interest and dividend income
- Military income
- Mortgage Credit Certificates
- Part-time job, second job, or seasonal income
- Performance-based RSUs or restricted stock income
- Rental income
- Self-employment income
- Social Security, VA, or other government retirement or annuity
- Time-based RSUs or restricted stock income (awarded in multiple consecutive years)

### 3-Year Continuance Documentation Required
Must document that income will continue for at least 3 more years:
- Alimony or child support
- Distributions from retirement accounts (401k, IRA, SEP, Keogh)
- Mortgage differential payments
- Notes receivable
- Public assistance
- Royalty payment income
- Social Security (not retirement or long-term disability)
- Time-based RSUs or restricted stock when receipt was a one-time event
- VA benefits (not retirement or long-term disability)

**Trust income**: Continuance requirement depends on the type of income received through the trust:
- Rental income through a trust = no continuance required
- Fixed payment from a depleting asset through a trust = 3-year continuance required

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## FHLMC — Continuance Framework

### No Continuance Documentation Required (presumed to continue)
- Base non-fluctuating employment earnings
- Fluctuating hourly employment earnings
- Commission income
- Bonus income
- Overtime income
- Restricted stock / RSUs subject to performance-based vesting
- Tip income
- Automobile allowance
- Military entitlements / Reserve or National Guard
- Unemployment (associated with seasonal employment)
- Self-employment income
- Royalty payments (two-year history)
- Foster care income
- Housing or parsonage allowance
- Mortgage Credit Certificate
- Rental income
- Tax-exempt income

_UW must confirm income is "likely to continue for at least the next three years" but is not required to obtain documentation to verify continuance, absent knowledge that income has ceased or will cease._

### Continuance Documentation Required
- Mortgage differential
- Notes receivable
- Royalty payments (one-year history — less established)
- Restricted stock / RSUs subject to time-based vesting provisions
- Trust income (fixed payment)

_Documentation: Duration of payments must be confirmed._

### Alimony / Child Support / Separate Maintenance
- Document duration of obligation (must continue at least 3 more years)

### Income with Possible Expiration (UW judgment required)
- Retirement income (SS, pension, annuity): UW must assess whether continuance is documentable based on knowledge of the specific benefit type, applicable law, and eligibility criteria
- Survivor/dependent benefits
- Long-term disability (SS, VA, workers' comp, private disability)
- SSI
- Public assistance (TANF)

_Pending or current re-evaluation of medical eligibility is NOT an indication that income will not continue._

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## Key Principle: Depleting Assets

> When income has a defined expiration date or depletes an asset, underwriters must consider the borrower's continued capacity to repay the mortgage when the income expires or the distributions will deplete the asset, **prior to maturation of the mortgage loan**.

This is especially important when the depleting income is the sole source or the majority of qualifying income. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## Documentation Approach

**When continuance IS required:**
- Obtain documentation showing the income will continue for at least 3 years
- Examples: alimony — court order showing children's ages and payment duration; retirement distributions — account balance statement showing sufficient assets remain

**When continuance is NOT required:**
- State in the loan analysis that the income is on the indefinite-continuance list
- No additional documentation needed unless there is knowledge or evidence that the income will cease

## Common Pitfalls

- Requiring 3-year continuance documentation for rental income, self-employment, or base salary — these are on the indefinite list
- Failing to document 3-year continuance for alimony/child support — a common omission
- Not distinguishing SS retirement/LTD from other SS types (survivor, SSI) — different continuance rules apply
- Ignoring the depleting-asset concern when distributions from a 401k are the primary income source — the UW must consider what happens when the account is depleted before loan maturity
