## Summary

A borrower with 25% or more ownership interest in a business is self-employed. Two years of self-employment is required in most cases. Income is calculated differently depending on business structure: Schedule C (sole proprietor), Form 1065 (partnership), Form 1120S (S-corp), or Form 1120 (C-corp). The business must be verified through a third-party source within 120 days of the Note date.

## Key Rules

- A borrower with **25% or more** ownership in any business — sole proprietorship, partnership, or corporation — is considered self-employed. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
- A **two-year history** of self-employment is required in most instances to confirm income stability. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
- Business existence must be verified through a **third-party source within 120 calendar days** prior to the Note date (FNMA and FHLMC). (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
- If the borrower has been self-employed **less than two years**, income may still be considered if the borrower has a two-year history of earning income at the same or greater level in the same or similar occupation. Use the **lesser** of the new business income or prior occupation income. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
- If the business structure changes (e.g., sole prop to S-corp), the prior and current businesses must be treated as **different businesses** unless there is no change in ownership percentage and no change in products, services, or location. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FNMA — One Year of Returns (DU exception)
DU may permit one year of personal and business returns when ALL of the following are met:
- Business has been in existence for at least **five years** as reflected on the loan application
- Borrower has had **25%+ ownership for the last five consecutive years**
- Individual and business returns reflect at least **12 months** of SE income from current business
- Self-Employment Income Worksheet is fully completed
(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FNMA — Business Returns May Be Waived
When two years of signed individual returns are provided, business returns may be waived if:
- Borrower is using **personal funds** for down payment, closing costs, and reserves
- Borrower has been SE in the **same business for at least 5 years**
- Individual returns show **increasing** SE income over the past two years
(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## Documentation Requirements by Entity Type

### All Self-Employed Borrowers
- Self-Employment Income Worksheet (loanDepot required)
- Third-party business verification (within 120 days of Note date)
- IRS Form 4506-C / tax transcripts per policy

### Sole Proprietor (Schedule C)
| Business Age | FHLMC Required | FNMA Required |
|-------------|---------------|---------------|
| > 5 years | 1 year personal (1040 + Sch C) | 2 years personal; or 1 year if DU exception met |
| ≤ 5 years | 2 years personal (1040 + Sch C) | 2 years personal |

### Partnership (Form 1065 + Schedule K-1)
| Business Age | FHLMC Required | FNMA Required |
|-------------|---------------|---------------|
| > 5 years | 1 year personal + 1 year 1065 + K-1s | 2 years personal + 2 years 1065 + K-1s |
| ≤ 5 years | 2 years personal + 2 years 1065 + K-1s | 2 years personal + 2 years 1065 + K-1s |

### S Corporation (Form 1120S + Schedule K-1)
| Business Age | FHLMC Required | FNMA Required |
|-------------|---------------|---------------|
| > 5 years | 1 yr personal + 1 yr 1120S + K-1s + Form 1125-E + W-2s | 2 yrs personal + 2 yrs 1120S + K-1s |
| ≤ 5 years | 2 yrs personal + 2 yrs 1120S + K-1s + Form 1125-E + W-2s | 2 yrs personal + 2 yrs 1120S + K-1s |

### C Corporation (Form 1120)
- FNMA: Corporate earnings may only be used if borrower **owns 100%** of the business.
- FHLMC: Underwriter must determine borrower's ownership percentage; not restricted to 100%.
- Required: Personal returns + Form 1120 + Form 1125-E + W-2s (1 or 2 years based on business age per FHLMC; 2 years per FNMA standard)
(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## Partnership Income — Access to Funds

Income from partnerships can only be considered if the UW documents:
- The income was **actually distributed** to the borrower, OR
- The business has **adequate liquidity** to support the withdrawal of earnings
- If a two-year history of "guaranteed payments to the partner" exists, those can be added to cash flow directly
- If the Schedule K-1 reflects a documented, stable history of cash distributions consistent with the income level used to qualify → no additional liquidity documentation required
- **FHLMC**: Cash distributions on Schedule K-1 may NOT be used as qualifying income (only to establish liquidity/access to business funds)
(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## S-Corp Income — Access to Funds

- Ordinary income, net real estate income, and other net rental income from Schedule K-1 may be included if the business has adequate liquidity
- Two-year history of guaranteed payments → no further liquidity documentation needed
- S-corp cash distributions used to establish access: must be reasonably consistent with ordinary income
(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## S-Corp Cash Flow Adjustments

**Add back to business cash flow:**
- Depreciation
- Depletion
- Amortization
- Casualty losses
- Other non-recurring losses

**Subtract from business cash flow:**
- Travel and meals exclusion
- Non-recurring income
- Total obligations on mortgages/notes/bonds payable in less than one year (unless lines of credit that roll regularly, or business has sufficient liquid assets)
(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## Exceptions & Nuances

- **Business structure change** — a switch from sole prop to S-corp or S-corp to C-corp triggers treatment as a different business unless no other changes occurred. Requires documented explanation.
- **Relocation** — If the borrower is moving to a different geographic area, the UW must consider market acceptance of the business in the new location and document why income will continue.
- **DU validation (FNMA Schedule C)** — If DU validates Schedule C SE income, tax returns are not required. Follow DU message for documentation (transcript may substitute).
- **FHLMC LPA** — Borrowers employed by a family-owned business are not eligible for LPA automated income assessment using employer data (may use account data). Signed personal return for the most recent year still required.

## Common Pitfalls

- Including corporate income (Form 1120) when borrower owns less than 100% on FNMA loans
- Not verifying business existence through a qualifying third-party source (CPA letter, state licensing, business website — document the method used)
- Using partnership K-1 income without confirming distribution history or business liquidity
- Treating a business structure change as the same business without verifying all conditions are met
- Missing that FHLMC prohibits K-1 cash distributions as qualifying income (common FNMA vs. FHLMC error)
- Not applying income trending analysis when income is declining year-over-year

## Interpretation Notes

> ⚠️ **Interpretation**: Content below is not directly sourced. It reflects practical application or professional judgment.

- In practice, the "business has adequate liquidity" standard for S-corp/partnership access to funds is often met by showing that the business bank statements reflect cash equal to at least the annual amount being used to qualify. This is a common industry standard but is not defined precisely in loanDepot's guide.
- For the FNMA 1-year return exception (5-year business, 5-year ownership), DU must specifically issue a 1-year recommendation. An underwriter cannot apply this exception on a manual basis outside of DU.
