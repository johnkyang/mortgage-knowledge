## Summary

Credit card financing is permitted for certain early-application fees (appraisal, credit report, lock-in, origination, commitment fees) up to defined limits. Credit card advances for the **down payment** are never permitted. Reward points may be used if converted to cash prior to closing.

## Key Rules

### Credit Card Financing — Permitted Use

- Credit card charges for common and customary fees paid **outside of closing** (lock-in fees, origination fees, commitment fees, credit report fees, appraisal fees) are permitted because these are not extraordinary amounts and the resulting credit card debt is included in the borrower's DTI. Borrowers are not required to pay off these charges before closing. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
- **Under no circumstances may credit card financing be used for the down payment.** (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FNMA — Credit Card Financing Limits

Credit card financing for pre-closing fees is allowed up to a **maximum of 2% of the loan amount**, provided either:
- The borrower has sufficient liquid funds (in addition to other closing costs and down payment) to cover the charges, **or**
- The credit card payment is recalculated per Selling Guide B3-6-05 (Monthly Debt Obligations), with the updated payment included in the qualifying ratio.

**DU treatment**: Apply manually by either adding the fees to Line F (Estimated Closing Costs) in the Details of Transaction and removing any Borrower Paid Fees from Other Credits, **or** by increasing the monthly credit card payment in the Liabilities section to include the charges. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FHLMC — Credit Card Financing Limits

The maximum amount charged or advanced may not exceed the **greater of 2% of the mortgage amount or $1,500**, and either:
- The borrower has sufficient verified funds to pay these fees (in addition to qualifying funds), and the borrower is not required to pay off charges at closing, **or**
- The amount charged is included in total outstanding debt with repayment factored into DTI per FHLMC Seller Guide Section 5401.2.

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### Reward Points — FNMA

Credit card reward points are acceptable for closing costs, down payments, and reserves if **converted to cash prior to closing**:
- If converted to cash and **deposited** into borrower's depository account: no additional documentation required unless it's a large deposit.
- If converted but **not deposited**: provide evidence the reward points were available prior to conversion (e.g., credit card reward statement) and converted to cash before closing.
- **DU input**: Enter cash value of reward points as an asset with account type "Other" and description "Other Liquid" if not yet converted and deposited. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### Reward Points — FHLMC

Reward points must be redeemed for cash:
- **Not yet deposited**: Provide evidence of borrower ownership and cash value, and evidence points were redeemed for cash prior to closing.
- **Redeemed and deposited**: Follow FHLMC deposit evaluation requirements per Seller Guide 5501.3(a)(iii). (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## Exceptions & Nuances

- Refer to the [[large-deposits]] section when reward point conversions result in a deposit exceeding 50% of monthly qualifying income.
- Credit card advances (beyond the permitted fee categories) remain ineligible — see [[ineligible-assets]].
