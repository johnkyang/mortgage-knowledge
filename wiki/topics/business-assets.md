## Summary

Business assets may be used as a source of funds for down payment, closing costs, prepaids, and reserves when the borrower is self-employed — subject to a cash flow analysis demonstrating the withdrawal will not harm the business's ability to operate.

## Key Rules

### General Requirements (FNMA and FHLMC)

- Business assets are eligible only when the borrower is **self-employed** and the underwriter has evaluated individual (and if applicable, business) federal income tax returns. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
- The impact of withdrawal on the business's ability to continue operating must be considered in the underwriting analysis. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
- Documentation may require several months of recent business account statements to evaluate cash flow needs and trends over time, or a current balance sheet. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
- The loan file must contain a **written cash flow analysis and conclusions** using individual and/or business tax returns. Any cash flow analysis format may be used that enables the underwriter to determine the withdrawal will not have a detrimental effect on the business. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
- **Exception**: When self-employed income is not being used to qualify the borrower, the required cash flow analysis may be waived. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
- The borrower must be listed as an owner of the business asset account, and the account must be verified per standard asset verification guidelines. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FHLMC-Specific

- Business assets are eligible as borrower personal funds when both FHLMC Seller Guide Sections 5501.3(b)(iv) and 5304.1(f) requirements are met. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
- Large deposit documentation is **not required** for business accounts, provided the underwriter reviews a minimum of the most recent **two months** of business account statements and determines the deposits are typical for the borrower's business. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])
