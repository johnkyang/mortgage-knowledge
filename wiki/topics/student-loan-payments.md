## Overview

Student loans in repayment, deferment, or forbearance must be included in DTI. The qualifying payment varies by GSE and repayment plan type. FHLMC does not permit a $0 student loan payment. (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.270–272)

---

## Key Rules

### FNMA — Student Loan Payment Determination

| Scenario | Payment to Use |
|---|---|
| Credit report reflects a monthly payment | Use that amount |
| Credit report does not reflect the correct payment | Use the payment on the most recent student loan statement |
| Credit report shows $0 or no payment; borrower is on income-driven repayment (IDR) | Obtain documentation confirming $0 actual payment; $0 may be used |
| Deferred or in forbearance | Use the **greater of**: 1% of outstanding balance OR a fully amortizing payment based on documented loan repayment terms |

(Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.270–271)

### FHLMC — Student Loan Payment Determination

- (Lender note: In all cases, an amount greater than zero must be included in DTI for all student loans — $0 is never acceptable) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.271)

| Scenario | Payment to Use |
|---|---|
| Reported payment > $0 | Use the payment reported on the credit report or other file documentation |
| Reported payment = $0 | Use **0.5% of the outstanding loan balance** |

(Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.271)

### FHLMC — Student Loan Forgiveness / Cancellation / Discharge / Employment-Contingent Repayment

The debt may be excluded from DTI when: (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.271)
- 10 or fewer monthly payments remain until the full balance will be forgiven/canceled/discharged/paid, OR
- The current payment is deferred or in forbearance AND the full balance will be forgiven/canceled/discharged/paid at the end of that period

AND:
- The borrower currently meets the requirements for the forgiveness/cancellation/discharge/employment-contingent repayment program, with no indication in the file that the borrower will become ineligible

- (Lender note: The loan file must contain documentation of eligibility or approval from the student loan program or employer, as applicable) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.271)

### FHLMC — Income-Driven Repayment (IDR) Plans — Special Cases

When file documentation indicates the borrower must recertify income OR the payment will increase prior to the first mortgage payment: (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.271–272)

Use the **greatest of**:
- Current payment OR 0.5% of outstanding balance
- Documented future payment amount if greater than current payment
- Future payment amount less than or equal to current payment, IF the borrower has re-certified income and the future payment is approved (must be > $0)

(Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.272)

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ The FNMA vs. FHLMC difference on IDR/$0 payments is significant. FNMA allows a $0 payment if the borrower can document their IDR plan results in $0. FHLMC requires at least 0.5% of the balance — often $200–500+/month for borrowers with large student loan balances. This materially affects DTI and program eligibility.

⚠️ FNMA's 1% option for deferred/forbearance loans (vs. a fully amortizing payment) is a borrower-friendly calculation. Choose 1% unless the fully amortizing payment would be lower.

⚠️ FHLMC's loan forgiveness exclusion requires current eligibility AND no indication the borrower will become ineligible — this is a judgment call. If the borrower's income is rising rapidly toward the forgiveness income threshold, this could be flagged.

⚠️ Employment-contingent repayment programs (e.g., Public Service Loan Forgiveness) are included in the forgiveness carve-out. Document the employer's participation and the borrower's enrollment.
