## Overview

Total monthly obligations include the qualifying payment for the subject mortgage and all other long-term and short-term monthly debts. All liabilities reflected on the application, credit report, paystubs, and other file documentation must be considered. (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.273–274)

---

## Key Rules

### Components of Total Monthly Obligations

The total monthly obligation is the sum of: (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.273–274)

**Housing payment for each borrower's principal residence:**
- If the subject loan is the borrower's principal residence: use the PITIA and qualifying payment amount
- If there is a non-occupant borrower: use the mortgage payment (including HOA fees and subordinate lien payments)
- For borrowers who pay rent: use the rent payment (see [[monthly-housing-expense]] for documentation)

**Subject property payment:**
- If the subject loan is for a second home or investment property: use the qualifying payment amount

**Monthly debt payments including:**
- Installment debts and other mortgage debts extending beyond 10 months
- Installment debts paying off in 10 months or fewer, **if the payments significantly affect the borrower's ability to meet credit obligations**
- Monthly payments on installment debts secured by virtual currency / cryptocurrency
- Revolving debts
- (Lender note: FHLMC only: Open-ended accounts — the full outstanding balance must be included in DTI, OR the company must verify sufficient funds from an eligible source to pay off the balance in addition to other qualifying funds) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.273)
- Lease agreements, regardless of expiration date
- Alimony, child support, or maintenance payments extending beyond 10 months
- Other recurring obligations
- Any net loss from a rental property

**Solar panel lease note:** (Lender note: Refer to the Properties with Solar Panels policy for requirements to exclude solar lease payments from DTI) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.274)

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ The short-term debt inclusion exception ("significantly affect ability to meet credit obligations") is a judgment call for debts with ≤10 months remaining. Underwriters should document the rationale when including or excluding borderline short-term debts.

⚠️ FHLMC's open-ended account rule (full balance in DTI unless funds verified) differs from FNMA's 30-day account treatment — see [[open-30-day-accounts]] for the specific mechanics.

⚠️ Lease payments are included regardless of expiration date — even if a car lease expires in 3 months, it is still in DTI. The only exception is solar panel leases, which follow a separate policy not yet ingested.

⚠️ Net rental loss (not gross rent) is added to total monthly obligations — if Schedule E shows a net loss on a rental property, that loss flows into DTI. Rental income that produces a positive net is handled on the income side.
