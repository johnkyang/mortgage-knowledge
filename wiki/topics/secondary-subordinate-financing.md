## Overview

Secondary financing is all financing subordinate in lien priority to the first mortgage. Terms must be disclosed to the appraiser and mortgage insurer. Community Seconds / Affordable Seconds / DPA programs must be listed in the AFH Program Directory to be eligible. (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.269–270)

---

## Key Rules

### Disclosure Requirements

- (Lender note: Terms of any secondary financing — including Note rate and institution or individual providing the financing — must be disclosed to the appraiser and mortgage insurer, as applicable) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.269)

### FNMA — Acceptable Subordinate Financing

Acceptable secondary financing includes: (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.269)
- Mortgages with regular payments covering at least the interest due (no negative amortization)
- Mortgages with deferred payments in connection with employer subordinate financing
- Mortgages with interest at a market rate
- Variable payment mortgages complying with the above criteria

- (Lender note: If seller-provided financing is more than 2% below current standard rates for second mortgages, the subordinate financing must be treated as a sales concession and deducted from the sales price) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.269)
- (Lender note: Required documentation: Copy of the Note, Security Instrument, and subordination agreement — if applicable) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.269)
- (Lender note: Shared appreciation is NOT permitted unless the subordinate financing is a qualified Community Second) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.269)

### FHLMC — New Secondary Financing Requirements

Secondary financing originated concurrently with the first lien must meet: (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.269)
- Maturity date or amortization basis of the junior lien must not be less than 5 years after the first lien Note date, unless the junior is fully amortizing or a HELOC
- The junior lien must not contain a call provision within the 5-year period, unless the junior is a HELOC

**Employer Assisted Homeownership (EAH) Benefit Exception:**
- EAH secondary financing terms may not require repayment in full unless: the borrower terminates employment for any reason, OR the employer terminates the borrower (for reasons other than long-term disability, position elimination, or reduction-in-force) (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.270)
- Terms must provide for regular monthly payments sufficient to meet interest due; interest may not accrue (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.270)
- If EAH monthly P&I begins on or after the 61st payment under the first lien, OR if repayment of principal is due only upon sale or default: the EAH monthly payment may be excluded from the housing expense and DTI ratios. Otherwise, the payment must be included in both ratios (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.270)

**FHLMC Documentation Requirements:**
- Note or other evidence of subordinate lien terms
- Settlement/Closing Disclosure statement or alternative form evidencing fees and costs paid at closing for the secondary financing
- For HELOCs: the HELOC agreement indicating all fees, costs, and the maximum permitted credit advance

### FHLMC — Existing Secondary Financing Requirements

- (Lender note: Evidence of subordination of the outstanding secondary financing must be in the loan file) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.270)
- (Lender note: The junior lien must have scheduled payments to meet the interest due) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.270)

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ The FNMA seller-financing 2% below market rate rule triggers a sales concession treatment — underwriters must compare the subordinate note rate to current second mortgage market rates at the time of origination to determine if this applies.

⚠️ FHLMC's 5-year minimum term rule applies at origination — if the junior lien has a balloon or call within 5 years, it fails unless it's a HELOC or fully amortizing. This is a common issue with seller-carry seconds on investment properties.

⚠️ The EAH benefit DTI exclusion (61st payment or sale/default trigger) is FHLMC-specific and common in employer relocation programs. The key test is whether regular P&I payments begin within the first 60 months — if they do, the payment must be included in DTI.
