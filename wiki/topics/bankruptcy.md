## Overview

Bankruptcy is a significant derogatory credit event that imposes a waiting period before a borrower is eligible for a new Fannie Mae loan. Waiting periods differ by bankruptcy chapter and whether extenuating circumstances apply. Both DU and manual underwriting must satisfy these waiting periods, though DU measures from the credit report date rather than the disbursement date. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-07)

---

## Key Rules

### Chapter 7 and Chapter 11 Bankruptcy

| Condition | Waiting Period |
|---|---|
| Standard | **4 years** from discharge or dismissal date |
| Extenuating circumstances documented | **2 years** from discharge or dismissal date |

(Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-07)

### Chapter 13 Bankruptcy

A distinction is made between Chapter 13 bankruptcies that were **discharged** (completed plan) and those that were **dismissed** (plan not completed):

| Condition | Waiting Period |
|---|---|
| Discharged Chapter 13 (standard and extenuating circumstances) | **2 years** from discharge date — no exception permitted |
| Dismissed Chapter 13 (standard) | **4 years** from dismissal date |
| Dismissed Chapter 13 (extenuating circumstances) | **2 years** from dismissal date |

The shorter waiting period after discharge recognizes that the borrower has already demonstrated financial responsibility through the successful completion of the repayment plan. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-07)

### Multiple Bankruptcy Filings

When a borrower has more than one bankruptcy filing within the past 7 years:

| Condition | Waiting Period |
|---|---|
| Standard | **5 years** from most recent dismissal or discharge date |
| Extenuating circumstances (most recent filing must be EC-related) | **3 years** from most recent discharge or dismissal date |

**Important**: Two borrowers each with one individual bankruptcy do NOT constitute multiple bankruptcies. Multiple bankruptcies means one borrower with more than one filing. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-07)

### Bankruptcy and Foreclosure on the Same Mortgage

If a mortgage debt was discharged through bankruptcy, the **bankruptcy waiting periods** may apply, provided the lender obtains documentation confirming the mortgage obligation was discharged. Otherwise, the **greater of** the applicable bankruptcy or foreclosure waiting period must be applied. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-07)

### Measurement of the Waiting Period

- Waiting period begins on the **discharge or dismissal date** (as applicable)
- Waiting period ends on the **disbursement date** of the new loan (manual UW)
- For DU: DU uses the **credit report date** to measure; lender may use the disbursement date to confirm the period has been met if the credit report date produces an inaccurate result

(Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-07, B3-5.3-09)

### Re-establishing Credit After Bankruptcy

After a bankruptcy, credit is considered re-established when ALL of the following are met:

1. The applicable waiting period and additional requirements are met
2. The loan receives a DU Approve/Eligible recommendation or, if manually underwritten, meets minimum credit score requirements
3. The borrower has **traditional credit** — nontraditional credit or "thin files" are not acceptable for re-establishing credit after a derogatory event

(Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-07)

### DU Handling of Bankruptcies

DU applies the following logic:

- **Chapter 13 discharged within 2 years**, dismissed within 4 years, or filed but neither discharged nor dismissed within 4 years → **Ineligible recommendation**
- **Non-Chapter 13 filed, discharged, or dismissed within 4 years** → **Ineligible recommendation**
- DU does not take bankruptcy public records more than **7 years old** into account
- DU does not automatically identify multiple filings — it issues a message when multiple filings may exist, requiring the lender to confirm

**Correcting Inaccurate Bankruptcy on Credit Report:**
Enter `Confirmed CR BK Incorrect` in the online loan application to instruct DU to disregard the bankruptcy. The lender must then document that the Ch. 13 was discharged ≥2 years or dismissed ≥4 years, or that a non-Ch. 13 bankruptcy was discharged or dismissed ≥4 years from disbursement.

**Bankruptcy Due to Extenuating Circumstances:**
Enter `Confirmed CR BK EC` to instruct DU to disregard the bankruptcy. The lender must document: EC was the cause, AND Ch. 13 dismissed ≥2 years or non-Ch. 13 discharged or dismissed ≥2 years from disbursement. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-09)

## Exceptions & Nuances

### Timeshare Accounts

Timeshare accounts are considered installment loans and are **not subject to bankruptcy waiting periods** even if reported as mortgage loans. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-07)

### High LTV Refinance Loans

Significant derogatory credit event waiting period requirements (including bankruptcy) are **not applicable** to high LTV refinance loans (see B5-7-02). (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-07)

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ There is NO extenuating circumstances exception for a Chapter 13 discharge (the waiting period is already 2 years and cannot be reduced further). EC only helps for Chapter 13 dismissals (reduces 4 years to 2 years).

⚠️ DU uses the credit report date, not the disbursement date, to calculate the waiting period. If the credit report date produces an "Ineligible" but the disbursement date would clear the period, the lender may manually confirm eligibility — but this requires documentation.

⚠️ After bankruptcy, nontraditional credit does not satisfy the re-establishing credit requirement. The borrower must have traditional tradelines and a credit score.

⚠️ Debts not satisfied by bankruptcy must be paid off or have an established repayment schedule before the loan can close. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-07)
