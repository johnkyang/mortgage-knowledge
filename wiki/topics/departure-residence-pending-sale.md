## Overview

If the borrower has not sold their existing residence — or if it has been sold but title has not transferred — the payment for the existing residence must be included in qualification. It may be excluded from DTI under specific conditions. (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.259)

---

## Key Rules

### Default Rule

- (Lender note: The payment for the existing departure residence must be included in DTI until either the sale is complete with title transferred, or exclusion conditions are met) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.259)

### Conditions to Exclude the Departure Residence Payment from DTI

The monthly payment may be excluded if one of the following is provided: (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.259)

**Option 1: Executed Sales Contract**
- An executed sales contract for the current residence pending sale that confirms any financing contingencies have been cleared

**Option 2: Employee Relocation — FNMA**
- An executed buyout agreement as part of an employer relocation plan, where the employer/relocation company takes responsibility for the outstanding mortgage

**Option 2: Employee Relocation — FHLMC**
- If the relocation program includes a buyout agreement for purchase of the borrower's current primary residence, and either:
  - The buyout agreement is executed by the borrower, OR
  - The buyout agreement is not yet executed, but the seller maintains a signed statement from the borrower indicating their intention to accept the buyout. Additionally ONE of the following must also apply:
    - The borrower has sufficient reserves (in addition to other required reserves) to pay the monthly payment until the buyout agreement expiration date, OR
    - The documented relocation program terms include a provision that the borrower's employer will make the monthly payments until the property is sold

(Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.259)

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ The executed sales contract must confirm that financing contingencies have been cleared — a contract still subject to the buyer's financing is not sufficient to exclude the payment. Look for a contingency-release addendum or the financing contingency removal date.

⚠️ FHLMC's unexecuted buyout agreement path is more complex than FNMA's — it requires a signed statement from the borrower AND either a reserve cushion or employer payment provision. This is commonly seen in corporate relocation scenarios.

⚠️ The departure residence payment and rental income treatment interact: if the borrower converts the departing residence to a rental (rather than selling or relocation), the rental income and mortgage offset rules apply instead. See [[rental-income]] for the conversion-to-rental rules.
