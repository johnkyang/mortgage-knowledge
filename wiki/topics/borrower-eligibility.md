## Overview

Borrowers must be natural persons who have reached the age at which a mortgage Note can be legally enforced in the jurisdiction where the property is located. There is no maximum age limit. (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## Key Rules

### Who Qualifies as a Borrower
- A **borrower** is any applicant whose credit is used for qualifying purposes.
- A **co-borrower** is any borrower other than the one whose name appears first on the Note.
- Each borrower's identity must be confirmed prior to the extension of credit.

### Exceptions to the Natural Person Requirement
- **Inter vivos revocable trusts** — permitted
- **Land trusts** — permitted only in states where the beneficiary is an individual and where allowed per guidelines
- **Blind trusts** — **ineligible**

### SSN / ITIN Requirements
- Every borrower must have a valid U.S. Social Security Number (SSN) **or** a valid, unexpired Individual Tax ID Number (ITIN).
- An ITIN borrower who subsequently obtains a valid SSN **must** apply using the SSN — the ITIN may no longer be used.
- Canadian Social Insurance Numbers are **not permitted** in Empower or on any loan documents.

### SSN Validation
When SSN inconsistencies exist (number not issued, age/issue date discrepancies, SSN associated with a deceased individual), the SSN must be validated with the Social Security Administration (SSA):
- Direct validation by a qualified third-party vendor going directly to the SSA is acceptable.
- **SSA-89** (Authorization for SSA to Release SSN Verification) must be used.
- Standard vendor reports that are not direct SSA validations do **not** satisfy this requirement.

**FNMA only:** Upon positive SSN validation with the SSA where a discrepancy was identified, **Special Feature Code (SFC) 162** is required at loan delivery.

### SSN Ineligibility Triggers
A loan is **ineligible** if:
- The SSN cannot be validated with the SSA.
- The borrower's SSN format is invalid and they cannot provide a valid SSN.
- File evidence shows the borrower previously used an invalid SSN on qualifying documents (pay stubs, W-2s, WVOEs, tax returns).

### Establishing Ownership Interest
The borrower must establish ownership interest in the security property by:
1. Signing the security instrument
2. Signing the mortgage or deed of trust Note
3. Taking title to the property in the name of the individual borrowers

### Life Estates
If the security property is held in a life estate:
- The **life tenant** (right to use the property during lifetime) **must** be a borrower.
- **Remaindermen** (individuals who receive full ownership upon the life tenant's death) may be co-borrowers.
- All — the life tenant **and** all remaindermen — must sign the security instrument.

### Homebuyer Education (FNMA)
At least one borrower must complete homeownership education prior to closing for:
- All borrowers relying **solely on nontraditional credit** to qualify (regardless of loan product or first-time homebuyer status)
- **HomeReady purchase transactions** where all occupying borrowers are first-time homebuyers
- **Purchase transactions with LTV/CLTV/HCLTV > 95%** where all borrowers are first-time homebuyers

Provider must be a qualified third-party independent of the lender; content must align with NIS or HUD standards. Housing counseling is an acceptable alternative to homeownership education. The underwriter must confirm course content and retain the certificate of completion.

## Exceptions & Nuances

- Life estate and land trust eligibility is state-specific — confirm applicable state law.
- SFC 162 is FNMA-specific; FHLMC does not have an equivalent requirement per this guide.
- Homebuyer education requirements above are FNMA-sourced. FHLMC requirements are covered in program-specific guidelines.
