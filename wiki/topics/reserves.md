## Summary

Reserves are borrower assets remaining after the mortgage closes. They are measured in months of the monthly payment amount (PITIA). The required amount depends on transaction type, occupancy, number of units, and number of financed properties. DU or LPA determines reserve requirements for AUS loans. Funds to close are subtracted from available assets before calculating sufficient reserves.

## Key Rules

### What Reserves Measure

Reserves are measured by the number of months of the **monthly payment amount**, defined as the sum of:
- Principal and interest on the mortgage
- Property hazard insurance premiums
- Real estate taxes
- When applicable: mortgage insurance premiums, leasehold payments, HOA dues (excluding unit utility charges), payments on secondary financing

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### What Cannot Be Counted as Reserves

The following are **ineligible** as reserves:
- Funds that have not been vested
- Funds that cannot be withdrawn except upon retirement, employment termination, or death
- Stock held in an unlisted corporation
- Non-vested stock options and non-vested restricted stock
- Personal unsecured loans
- Interested party contributions (IPCs)
- Cash proceeds from a cash-out refinance on the subject property

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FNMA — Reserve Determination

Minimum required reserves vary based on:
- The transaction type
- Occupancy status and amortization type of the subject property
- Number of units in the subject property
- Number of other financed properties owned by the borrower

**DU loan casefiles**: DU determines reserve requirements based on overall risk assessment of the loan, the minimum requirement for the transaction, and multiple financed property considerations. When a borrower has multiple financed properties and is financing a second home or investment property, DU bases reserve calculations for other financed properties on the number of financed properties it determines.

**High LTV refinance loans**: Exempt from minimum reserve requirements.

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

### FHLMC — Reserve Determination

- For LPA mortgages, all reserves required by LPA must be verified as stated on the Feedback Certificate.
- When processing multiple mortgage applications for the same borrower, the **same assets may be used to meet reserve requirements for each mortgage transaction**.
- For Home Possible mortgages, refer to Section 4501.10 for minimum reserve requirements.

(Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]])

## Exceptions & Nuances

- Retirement account funds used for reserves are **not required to be withdrawn** from the accounts — they count at face value without liquidation.
- Gift funds may or may not be eligible for reserves depending on program and donor (see [[gift-funds]]).
- IPCs are explicitly ineligible for reserves — seller concessions cannot be structured to fund post-closing reserves.
