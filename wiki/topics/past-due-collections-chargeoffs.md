## Overview

For non-mortgage past due accounts, collections, and charge-offs, FNMA policy varies by occupancy type. FHLMC defers to LPA Accept findings. Medical collections receive favorable treatment under FNMA. (Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.324–325)

---

## Key Rules

### FNMA — By Occupancy Type

- (Lender note: **Past due accounts (not reported as collections)** must be brought current. Medical collection accounts are excluded from this requirement and are not required to be paid in full at or prior to closing) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.324)

| Occupancy / Property Type | Collections & Non-Mortgage Charge-Off Policy |
|---|---|
| 1-unit, principal residence | Borrowers are NOT required to pay off outstanding collections or non-mortgage charge-offs, regardless of the amount |
| 2-4 unit owner-occupied and second home | Collections and non-mortgage charge-offs totaling more than **$5,000** must be paid in full prior to or at closing |
| Investment property | Individual accounts ≥ **$250**, or accounts totaling > **$1,000**, must be paid in full prior to or at closing |

(Lender note: Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.324)

### FHLMC — Collections and Charge-Offs

- (Lender note: Follow LPA Accept findings. LPA evaluates the borrower's credit reputation and determines if it is acceptable) (Source: [[source-loandepot-conventional-guide-2026-03]], p.324)

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ The FNMA 1-unit primary residence policy (no payoff required regardless of amount) is borrower-friendly and frequently misunderstood. Some originators incorrectly require payoff of large collections on primary residences. Per loanDepot's guide reflecting FNMA policy, no payoff is required.

⚠️ The $5,000 aggregate threshold for 2-4 unit and second homes applies to collections AND non-mortgage charge-offs combined — not each account individually.

⚠️ For investment properties, the per-account threshold ($250) and the aggregate threshold ($1,000) work together: any single account ≥$250 must be paid, AND if total is >$1,000, all must be paid.

⚠️ "Medical collection accounts" are excluded from the general past-due rule but may still be reviewed by DU as part of overall credit risk assessment. The exclusion means they need not be paid off — it does not mean they are invisible to the AUS.

⚠️ This section covers NON-MORTGAGE accounts. Mortgage charge-offs are subject to the derogatory credit event seasoning rules. See [[derogatory-credit-event-seasoning]].
