## Overview

For non-mortgage past due accounts, collections, and charge-offs, FNMA policy varies by occupancy type and underwriting method (DU vs. manual). Medical collections receive favorable treatment — they are excluded from most payoff requirements. This section covers non-mortgage accounts only; mortgage charge-offs are subject to derogatory event waiting periods (see [[derogatory-credit-event-seasoning]]). (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-09, B3-6-07)

---

## Key Rules

### Past-Due Accounts (Not Reported as Collections)

Accounts that are reported as past due (but not reported as collection accounts) must be brought current. **Medical collection accounts are excluded** from this requirement and are not required to be paid in full at or prior to closing. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-09)

### Collections and Non-Mortgage Charge-Offs — DU Loans

| Occupancy / Property Type | Policy |
|---|---|
| 1-unit, principal residence | Borrowers are **NOT required** to pay off outstanding collections or non-mortgage charge-offs, regardless of the amount |
| 2–4 unit owner-occupied and second home | Collections and non-mortgage charge-offs totaling more than **$5,000** must be paid in full prior to or at closing |
| Investment property | Individual accounts ≥ **$250** OR accounts totaling > **$1,000** must be paid in full prior to or at closing |

**Note**: If the lender marks a collection account "Paid By Close" in the online loan application, DU will issue a message in the Findings report stating the collection must be paid. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-09)

### Collections and Non-Mortgage Charge-Offs — Manual Underwriting Loans

Non-medical collection accounts and charge-offs on non-mortgage accounts do **not** have to be paid off at or prior to closing if:
- The balance of an **individual account is less than $250**, AND
- The **total balance of all such accounts is $1,000 or less**

Non-medical collection accounts and charge-offs exceeding these limits must be paid off at or prior to closing. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-6-07)

### Judgments and Liens

Open judgments and all outstanding liens in the Public Records section of the credit report must be paid off at or prior to closing. Documentation of satisfaction and verification of funds must be in the loan file. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-09)

### Delinquent Federal Taxes

Delinquent credit including taxes, judgments, and charge-offs of non-mortgage accounts must generally be paid at or prior to closing. For federal income tax installment agreements, different rules apply — see [[irs-installment-agreements]]. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-6-07)

## Exceptions & Nuances

### Medical Collections — Special Treatment

Medical collection accounts are excluded from:
- The past-due accounts "must bring current" requirement
- The DU and manual UW payoff thresholds above

Medical collections are not required to be paid in full at or prior to closing in any occupancy scenario. However, they are still visible to DU and may influence the overall risk assessment.

**Note**: Tradelines reported as medical debt are not shown in the DU disputed tradeline message — lenders are not required to investigate disputed medical tradelines. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-09)

### DU vs. Manual Underwriting Distinction

The DU table (by occupancy) and the manual UW table (by account balance thresholds) are different policies. For DU loans, refer to B3-5.3-09. For manually underwritten loans, refer to B3-6-07. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.3-09, B3-6-07)

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ The FNMA 1-unit primary residence policy (no payoff required regardless of amount) is borrower-friendly and frequently misunderstood. Some originators incorrectly require payoff of large collections on primary residences. Per the FNMA Selling Guide, no payoff is required for DU loans.

⚠️ The $5,000 aggregate threshold for 2–4 unit and second homes applies to collections AND non-mortgage charge-offs combined — not each account individually.

⚠️ For investment properties (DU), the per-account threshold ($250) and aggregate threshold ($1,000) work together: any single account ≥$250 must be paid, AND if the total is >$1,000, all must be paid.

⚠️ Medical collections are excluded from payoff requirements but are still reviewed by DU as part of credit risk assessment. Exclusion means no payoff required — it does not mean DU ignores them.

⚠️ Mortgage charge-offs are NOT covered by this section. Mortgage charge-offs trigger derogatory event waiting periods — see [[derogatory-credit-event-seasoning]] and [[short-sale-deed-in-lieu]].
