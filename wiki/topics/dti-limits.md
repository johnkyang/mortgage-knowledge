## Overview

The DTI ratio compares total monthly debt obligations (including the proposed housing payment) to total monthly qualifying income. Fannie Mae sets different maximum DTI ratios for manual underwriting vs. DU casefiles. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-6-02)

---

## Key Rules

### Maximum DTI Ratios

| Underwriting Method | Maximum DTI |
|---|---|
| Manually underwritten loans (standard) | 36% |
| Manually underwritten loans (with elevated credit score + reserves per Eligibility Matrix) | Up to 45% |
| DU loan casefiles | 50% |

(Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-6-02)

### Exceptions to Maximum DTI

The standard maximum DTI does not apply to:

- **Cash-out refinances**: Maximum may be lower for DU casefiles (see B2-1.3-03)
- **High LTV refinance loans**: No maximum DTI (except for Alternative Qualification Path, which caps at 45%)
- **Borrowers without credit scores**: Manual UW cap is 36% for no-score borrowers (not eligible for the 45% exception) (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-5.4-01)
- **Non-occupant borrowers**: The occupying borrower's DTI cap is lower than 45% for manual UW (see B2-2-04)
- **Government loans (FHA/VA/USDA)**: Follow the respective agency's DTI requirements

(Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-6-02)

### What Is Included in Total Monthly Obligations

The total monthly obligation includes the sum of:

- **Housing payment** for each borrower's principal residence (PITIA for subject property, or mortgage/rent for other properties)
- **Qualifying payment** on the subject loan if it is a second home or investment property
- Monthly payments on installment debts with more than 10 months remaining
- Monthly payments on installment debts with 10 months or fewer if they significantly affect the borrower's ability to repay
- Monthly payments on installment debts secured by virtual currency (always included regardless of remaining term)
- Monthly payments on revolving debts
- Monthly payments on lease agreements (regardless of lease expiration)
- Monthly alimony, child support, or maintenance payments with more than 10 months remaining (alimony may instead be deducted from income)
- Monthly payments for other recurring monthly obligations
- Any net loss from a rental property

(Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-6-02)

### What Is NOT Included in Total Monthly Obligations

Payroll-related deductions and obligations are not considered liabilities for DTI purposes:

- Federal, state, and local income taxes
- FICA or other retirement contributions (including 401(k) repayments)
- Commuting costs
- Union dues
- Voluntary deductions

(Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-6-01)

### Re-Underwriting After DTI Changes

If additional debt or reduced income is discovered after underwriting up to and including closing:

1. Document the additional debt or reduced income
2. If new subordinate debt on subject property exists → **must re-underwrite** regardless of impact
3. Recalculate DTI outside of DU
4. If recalculated DTI exceeds 45% (manual) or 50% (DU) → **loan is not eligible**
5. For manual UW: if DTI increases above 36% threshold, the loan must meet the Eligibility Matrix requirements for the 36%–45% DTI band (credit score + reserves)
6. Final signed loan application must include all income and debts

(Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-6-02)

## Exceptions & Nuances

### Manual UW 36% vs. 45% Threshold

The 36% cap is the standard, but FNMA allows up to 45% if the borrower meets the credit score AND reserve requirements in the Eligibility Matrix for that DTI band. The Eligibility Matrix should be consulted directly for current thresholds. (Source: [[source-fnma-selling-guide-2026-04]], B3-6-02)

## Interpretation Notes

⚠️ The DU 50% DTI cap is an absolute ceiling — DU will not issue an Approve/Eligible recommendation at DTI above 50%. In practice, DU becomes more restrictive long before 50%, particularly for borrowers with lower credit scores or limited reserves.

⚠️ The manual UW path to 45% requires BOTH the credit score AND the reserve requirements from the Eligibility Matrix to be met simultaneously. Meeting one but not the other limits the borrower to 36%.

⚠️ Lease payments are always included in DTI regardless of how many months remain. This is different from installment debt, which has a 10-month threshold. Don't confuse the two.

⚠️ Alimony paid is the only debt that can be handled as either a monthly liability OR a reduction in qualifying income. Child support and separate maintenance must be treated as monthly liabilities.
