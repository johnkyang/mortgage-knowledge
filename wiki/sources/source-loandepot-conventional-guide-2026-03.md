## Summary

loanDepot's internal conventional underwriting guide covering credit policy, income, assets, liabilities, property, credit, and specialty programs. Applies to both FNMA and FHLMC conventional products. Contains loanDepot overlays on top of GSE guidelines. Where the guide is silent, defer to the Fannie Mae Selling Guide or Freddie Mac Single-Family Guide.

**Important**: This is a lender-level document (trust level 4). Rules stated here represent loanDepot policy, which may be stricter than the underlying GSE guidelines. Not all lender overlays apply to all loan channels — guide references Retail, Joint Venture (JV), and Direct (LDW) channels separately.

## Key Excerpts

> The information contained in this Lending Guide represents loanDepot's overall credit philosophy and underwriting guidance. The primary focus of these guidelines is on loanDepot's Conventional products... Loans must comply with GSE requirements, Federal Housing Finance Agency (FHFA) loan limits and as well as loanDepot overlays, when applicable. Where differences exist between this Guide and the product matrices, default to the product matrices. Where policy is not stated in this Guide, refer to the following guideline resources regarding the topic in question: Fannie Mae Selling Guide, Freddie Mac Single-Family Guide...

> The appropriate income calculator, as defined below, is required to be used to calculate and analyze all sources of income. Wage Earner (excluding Restricted Stock) – Web-based calculator. Non-taxable, Rental, Self-Employed, Other Income – Excel calculator. Restricted Stock – standalone RSU Excel calculator.

> A borrower who has an ownership interest of 25% or more, in a business, is considered to be self-employed.
