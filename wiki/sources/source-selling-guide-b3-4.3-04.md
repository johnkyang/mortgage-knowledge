## Summary

This section governs the use of personal gift funds as a source of funds on Fannie Mae conventional loans. It defines eligible donors, occupancy-based restrictions, documentation requirements, and the distinction between gifts and loans.

## Key Excerpts

> Acceptable donors for personal gifts include a relative, defined as the borrower's spouse, child, or other dependent, or by any other individual who is related to the borrower by blood, marriage, adoption, or legal guardianship; or a fiancé, fiancée, or domestic partner.

> The gift funds must be deposited in the borrower's bank account before closing, or the donor may give the gift funds directly at the settlement.

> The gift donor may not be, or have any affiliation with, the builder, developer, real estate agent, or any other interested party to the real estate transaction.

> If the gift funds are used toward the down payment or minimum borrower contribution, the following is required:
> - A gift letter signed and dated by the donor and borrower that includes (1) the dollar amount of the gift; (2) the date the funds were transferred; (3) the donor's name, address, telephone number; (4) the donor's relationship to the borrower; and (5) a statement that no repayment is expected or implied either in the form of cash or future services.
> - Documentation evidencing the withdrawal and deposit of gift funds.

> Gifts are not permitted for investment properties.

> For a principal residence or second home, there is no minimum borrower contribution requirement from the borrower's own funds unless the LTV ratio is greater than 80 percent... [DU-determined requirements apply]

## Notes

- This section is subject to DU messaging override — DU findings may impose borrower contribution requirements even when the Selling Guide text suggests gifts are sufficient. Always check DU findings.
- Effective date should be verified against current Selling Guide at time of use. Lender Letters can update gift fund rules mid-year.
- The definition of "relative" for gift purposes is specific — not all "family" relationships qualify. Verify donor eligibility carefully.
